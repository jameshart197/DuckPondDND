The Dawnguard

The Dawnguard is the combined military might of the whole of Sogravia. Whilst officially led by the King, the primary leader of the Dawnguard is the Commander General. Below the Commander General, the Dawnguard is split into 6 subsections; The Defenders, Errants, Ventures, Encumbents, Cohorts and City Guards.

Each subsection has an individual leader (a General), and then 3 lieutenants (the Knight Captains). Below that are the Knights, of whom there can be many (there are over 400 Knight Defenders), or few (there are only 30 Knight Ventures). Below those are the general soldiers, then the soldiers within their first year (known as Green Soldiers), and finally the Squires who aspire to become soldiers.

The Dawnguard break down the army into legions (10,000 soldiers), legions can be broken into groups of 1000 soldiers, known as tribunes. A tribune can be further broken up into groups of 100 soldiers, known as companies. Companies can be broken up into groups of 10 soldiers, known as units. Each unit, company, tribune and legion will have a leader, who in turn reports to the leader of the larger group within which his group belongs, moving up the ranks until you reach the Commander General.

The Defenders

The Defenders make up the majority of members of the Dawnguard. They represent the standing army, soldiers defending the borders and fighting in the war with the Shadowveil. The Defenders have various units within them such as cavalry, archers, seige units, infantry and so on. It is primarily the Defenders that are supported by the Cohorts. Most Defenders are stationed at Kovach or Vanhania.

The Errants

The Errants are soldiers who mostly act on their own or in small groups. They are the soldiers that are sent to investigate disturbances around the country. They travel far and wide dealing with strange rumours, unusual occurences and issues within the peasantry. It is primarily Errants who would deal with any banditry or monster issues within Sogravia's borders. Errants are often strong independent soldiers used to seeing battle. They are often the least surprised by the occult, arcane and unusual.

The Ventures

The Ventures are the scouts. These soldiers almost always act as solos or groups of three. They are most renowned for their scouting missions into enemy territory, and many exploits are told of famous Ventures who made their way into the Shadowveil lands for scouting purposes, rescues or other daring missions. Ventures are the fewest in number in the Dawnguard, but have the highest death rates. Many Ventures are capable of flight in one way or another to aid them in scouting.

The Encumbents

The Encumbents are the royal guards, the heraldry, the army that is stationed at each city and outpost throughout Sogravia. Highly ceremonial, sometimes other Dawnguard disparage the Encumbents, saying they are not proper soldiers. However, perhaps due to the time they have spare, or perhaps due to the need to keep their reflexes sharp, many of the Encumbents practice in gladiator arenas and duels and are some of the best swordsmen in the land. There are many roles within the Encumbents that include extra resposibilities, such as the Heralds, musicians and Record Keepers who are a vital part of the Dawnguard for non-fighting purposes.

The Cohorts

Supplementary divisions within the Dawnguard, there are many cohorts, each with a specific purpose. The most widely known and well-regarded are the combat medics, engineers, sappers, arcane support and quartermaster cohorts. But there are countless more groups that contribute in various ways to the running, wellbeing and survival of the Dawnguard. These cohorts always have their own leaders and heirarchy, but each cohort will have a general who reports directly to the Commander General.

The City Guards

The City Guards are the police force that maintains the law within all the cities, towns and villages of Sogravia. They are part of the Dawnguard, and yet separate. Whilst most City Guards would take part in the defense of a city should it be attacked, for the most part they deal with petty crime, investigation and adjudication. There are as many lawyers and administrators in the City Guard as there are soldiers and policemen.

Races in Ael

Races are incredibly diverse within Ael. Due to the encoraching of the Shadowveil and the continuous war within the land of Listrus, there is little chance for races to keep to themselves and instead most races intermingle freely, resulting in a diluting of the racial stereotypes and behaviours. Sogravia is diverse and racism is almost unheard of within the nation. Instead, the only form of racial dominance or heirarchy is based on the longevity of certain races; whilst a Vedalken might not look down on a human, it is hard for someone who has lived a millenia to take the advice of someone who has lived barely thirty years.

Aaba on DNDBeyond

Aabas have very distinct features that obviously single them out as being much different than just another common humanoid. Atop their heads are two antennas that are used for searching for food, communication between other aaba, and can even enhance hearing. Their legs are the most prominent differences between other common races, for their legs are that of massive grasshoppers that are capable of carrying an Aaba up to 10 times its height. Landing is not an issue because their legs easily retract, acting like shocks to keep them upright.

Aaba skin ranges from forest green to a very dark brown. Female Aaba tend to be darker in shade than Males, but it can vary. Most Aaba have a pair of translucent wings that provide the gift of flight. However, many are unable to use them because of their curse. There are two types of Aabas, the Speedwings and the Chakraabas, the Speedwings are more martial in appearance and personality, the Chakraabas more magical.

Aarakocra on DNDBeyond

The Aarakocra mostly originate from the high up Eyries around Sogravia’s mountainous terrain. In modern days, despite still maintaining their Eyries in the mountains, they are the most valued of scouts and messengers throughout Sogravia. Given that they can fly for extended periods of time far above the many dangers of the land, the biggest threat to an Aarakocra is often the weather, and the most well outfitted messengers and couriers often have magical protection from weather and exhaustion.

Aasimar on DNDBeyond

It is said that the Aasimar of Feuralia are descended from Celestial beings, or were once influenced by those heavenly beings. Whether that is the case or not is unknown, but what is known is that from the Aasimar of Feuralia rose the Feurian Guard. Aasimar tend to be deeply religious, fanatic in their beliefs and quick to righteous fury. Aasimar often glow faintly with the light when their anger is roused, and can be almost blinding to look at when they unleash their celestial wings.

Autognome on DNDBeyond

In Sogravia, the practice of Warforging began as a means to shut out the Shadowveil influence. Despite being a somewhat barbaric ritual that can be incredibly painful for the person experiencing it, Warforging has been made popular by the rich and aristocratic, mostly to ensure their servants and bodyguards cannot be infiltrated by the Shadowveil.

In more recent times, the ever-creative gnomes discovered a less barbaric way of doing this, fusing themselves with an automated mechanical body to allow themselves to preserve their mind, whilst still retaining their memories and sense of self. Many gnomes have volunteered themselves to this process with the Magus Arcane.

Bugbears on DNDBeyond

Bugbears are an unassuming, peaceful race for the most part with roots in the Feywild. Often preferring lives as travelling alchemists, huntsmen and the like, many bugbears prefer a solitary lifestyle. Nonetheless, due to their stature and natural martial abilities, it is not rare to find bugbears as soldiers and guards in the employ of the rich and wealthy.

Centaurs on DNDBeyond

The Centaur Herds roam the hills and plains of the world and are one of the few races surviving almost entirely outside of the cities, always on the move. They go to the Hunt weekly and all centaurs that are able participate as the Herd hunts Elk, Wyverns, anything that is swift and a challenge. The Rite of the Hunt is sacred to them, and even Centaurs that venture elsewhere in the world will often feel the call to the Hunt.

The Centaur are a deeply reflective folk, and they worship the stars and constellations. Most Centaurs are deeply prideful and will brook no insults to themselves or their people, calling any such person who would throw insults to the Draw, a duel sacred in the Centaur culture.

Centaurs are very tribal and tend to dismiss other races as insignificant, their traditions bind them, but also keep them a little rural in comparison to many other races; Centaurs live on the move, and rarely stop in cities or towns, they trade little and they live a nomadic life. They tend to be very strong of arm, and have little tolerance for the old, weak or sickly. Centaurs come of age as young as 12, and live for many centuries. In Sogravia, most Centaur have joined the army as Cavalier units and outside of battle keep to their herds, well outside of cities. In Eldessa the Centaurs maintain their Nomadic lifestyle.

Changelings on DNDBeyond

Changelings throughout Ael are fairly rare, and have become even more so in Sogravia since the coming of the Shadowveil. Due to their ability to change their physical appearance to resemble another, many have associated them (falsely) with the Shadowveil, and many Changelings have fallen victim to propaganda.

Even prior to the coming of the Shadowveil, most Changelings prefer to keep to their “colourless” form and many Changelings find that they struggle with their personal identity. Changelings are highly valued for their skills as actors, spies and courtesans, but many Changelings who change form too often find themselves struggling to return to their “true” forms.

Dragonborn on DNDBeyond

Like Dragons themselves, Dragonborn are diverse, fierce and incredibly proud. It is said their features and racial traits come from blessings upon their lineage long ago, but these days their features are merely hereditary. Dragonborn are as varied as humans, and are diverse in their personalities and histories.

Many Dragonborn are alike to the Dragons that once blessed their lineage; White Dragonborns often come from the cold Northern stretches and mountains, Red Dragonborns often prefer sandy deserts and hot savannahs, etc.

These days however, most Dragonborn are found in cities just like other races, and though they are often quick-tempered, the Dragonborn are also quick to joy and laughter. Dragonborn are often mistaken for reptiles but are in fact warm-blooded draconic creatures with an internal temperature that is so hot as it may seem feverish to the human touch. Dragonborn often have deep seated traditions regarding personal honour.

Dryads on DNDBeyond

What few Dryads there are left in Sogravia tend to stick to the forests around Kovach, though many have carried saplings of their “soul-trees” from far further afield. Once found throughout the great forests all over Listrus, the distance from their soul-trees and the forced concentration of their race, as well as increased exposure to others, has resulted in the Dryads becoming incredibly scarce. Those Dryads that do interact with others tend to be playful, mischievous and shy.

Bound to a tree spirit themselves, rare is it that a Dryad would venture from the forest. However, it does happen on occasion; there was one travelling dryad renowned in Eldessa who carried a small sapling of her soul tree with her as she travelled the world teaching of the strange herbs and plants she had discovered.

Dwarves on DNDBeyond

Many of the Dwarves have become soldiers in the Dawnguard, due to their willingness to fight for their homeland, their excellent battle discipline and training and their naturally tough nature. The Dwarves do keep to their own home cities, with Thrakstun being the largest and most populous. Away from the armies, Dwarves are a clannish and independent group,with homes in mountains and hills all over the world. They are insular and solitary and not particularly welcoming of outsiders, though never truly turning anyone in need from their doorsteps.

Elves

Astral Elves on DNDBeyond

Astral Elves used to occupy the islands of Yulenta. There is a relatively small number of Astral Elves left within the lands of Sogravia, and those that do still survive after the coming of the Shadowveil, tend to be quiet, reclusive and live in isolation. Well known for their obsession with the stars and constellations of the night sky, and their contemplative lifestyles many Astral Elves are divination wizards with a light connection to the Magus Arcane for the purpose of knowledge shared.

Drow on DNDBeyond

The Drow are the only race who have managed to have somewhat peaceful interaction with the Shadowveil in Sogravia. There is said to be great conflict between the Drow and Shadowveil in the Underdark, but despite this the Drow seem to also have trade and understanding with them. Outside of Sogravia, the Drow are a dark and mistrusted race who are regularly described as innately evil, violent and corrupt.

The Drow are one of the few humanoid inhabitants of the Underdark. They have difficulty with sunlight and prefer only to come to the surface during the night or in places of shelter. There are, however, drow who trade with folk aboveground, and a few who make their names in the world above the ground have learnt to adapt to the sunlight. The cities of the Drow are said to be incredibly beautiful, though difficult to behold with human eyes.

Eladrin on DNDBeyond

The Eladrin, or seasonal Elves, have been a part of the land of Jazuc long before humans arrived and took over. Some Eladrin change seasons often, some change with the actual seasons, and some stay one season for centuries or even for life. The reason for these differences is unknown, even to the Eladrin themselves. Eladrin are as different and varied as the humans of the world.

High Elves on DNDBeyond

The Aristocracy of Elves, the High Elves are the city-bred elves of the common day and age. In Sogravia, they are often the higher-ups in the Magus Arcane. They tend towards arrogance and the belief that they are better than others, and most High Elves engage in at least some level of casual racism. High Elves tend to value their bloodlines and lineage more than most races. Oft proud and haughty, High Elves can often trace their bloodlines back for many millennia.

Wood Elves on DNDBeyond

Somewhat primitive and savage in comparison to the other Elves, they are still the Forest-Dwelling simpler Elves of old. Believing that they are best served by staying close to the Earth and nature, these Elves avoid the cities of modern civilization as they believe that the cities have moved away from the Gods and the Earth itself. Keeping close to nature the Wood Elves are often ritualistic and base much of their lives on the seasons and moons.

A proud but quiet folk they describe themselves as closest to the Earth of all the Elves and are often reproachful of those who adopt the city lifestyle. They spend much of their time singing, dancing and worshipping nature but do not be fooled; most Wood Elves are deadly swordsmen and exceptional archers with natural magic that they can bring to bear.

Races Continued

Fairies on DNDBeyond

Once such a rare sight that they were believed a myth in Sogravia, since the rise of the Shadowveil, the Fairies have mostly joined the Magus Arcane for both protection and mutual interest. Fairies are by nature gentle, curious and clever and are known for their natural magical affinity. Away from Sogravia, Fairies tend to live in places of natural beauty, be these waterfalls, lakes, forests or otherworldly caverns.

These small creatures tend to be solitary from the age of 9 or 10. They mature quickly although never truly maturing, and tend to be very nature based. Some small few have entered cities but they are few and far between. Usually a playful group that worship gods of laughter, joy, natural beauty and mischief, they are a gentle folk. They have no singular place of origin and tend not to have larger communities but instead smaller families dotted around. Most Fairies have inherent magic that they use to protect themselves despite their smaller stature, as most fairies stand between 3 and 4 feet tall. The nature of this magic, however, is usually the ability to hide and stay unnoticed when the larger folk come by.

Flumphs on DNDBeyond

Flumphs communicate telepathically. Though they resemble jellyfish, flumphs are sentient beings of great intelligence and wisdom, possessing advanced knowledge of religion, philosophy, mathematics, and countless other subjects. Flumphs are sensitive to the emotional states of nearby creatures. If a creature's thoughts suggest goodness, a flumph seeks that creature out. When facing creatures that exude evil, a flumph flees.

Air Genasi on DNDBeyond Earth Genasi on DNDBeyond Fire Genasi on DNDBeyond Water Genasi on DNDBeyond

The Genasi worship the Primordials as Gods, and as such have a very naturalistic essence at the core of their being. A water Genasi, for example, may for most of their lives operate as a normal inhabitant of a city, but will still retain a part of themselves that is truly elemental.

The Genasi have a tendency to marry within their chosen element, though it is possible for a Fire and Earth Genasi, for example, to breed. Should this occur, it is up to the twists of fate which element the child shall be born closest to. Genasi have spread across the whole world, and are second only to humans in their diversity and their variety of homeplaces.

Gnolls on DNDBeyond

Whether firmly as guards, bodyguards, soldiers or thugs, Gnolls usually find their way to violent careers and lifestyles for gnolls can be best described as aggressive, furry powerhouses. They can range anywhere from aggressively friendly, aggressively dogmatic, aggressively unimpressed, and just about any state of being you can stick ‘aggressive’ in front of.

Gnomes on DNDBeyond

Gnomes are spread throughout the world, and can vary greatly in their nature; nevertheless there is one trait that can link them all together, and that is curiosity. Gnomes are often found as students of the various arts, be they magical or scientific. They are a people who love to create, and to build, and the cities that gnomes have built tend to be incredibly complicated and magnificent to behold (though not always particularly organised!).

The Forest Gnomes tend towards a more peaceful life, and are usually found dwelling on the edges of forests. The Rock Gnomes tend towards the crystalline groves at the foothills of mountains, and tend to be more of the magical arts. The Deep Gnomes tend towards the Underdark and live further from the rest of society than other gnomes. Less is known about these gnomes, for they venture rarely to the surface, but it is said they are the most incredible of builders, and have a symbiotic relationship with the Earth.

Goliath on DNDBeyond

Goliaths are as cold and harsh as the lands in which they live, with voices that rumble like the mountains themselves and skin hardened to almost rock-like. They prefer colder climes but adapt well to wherever they find themselves. Goliaths are proud and have rigid codes of honour. Due to their stature and strength they are often found either working in construction and manual labour or as guards and soldiers. In the armies of Sogravia, Goliaths are the most valued of foot soldiers and are often quickly promoted to Captains and Legionnaires.

Hadozee on DNDBeyond

Exiled from their jungles by the coming of the Shadowveil, Hadozee in Sogravia are a somewhat tribal folk who keep to the edges of the forests surrounding Kovatch. There are not many of them left, and those that are left tend towards shyness and isolation. There are, however, a unit of Hadozee in the Sogravian army who are at once both terrifying and dangerous, functioning as a somewhat guerilla warband within the army. Away from Sogravia, Hadozee are a mostly peaceful tribal folk that keep to the upper reaches of the jungles and rainforests of Jazuc.

Half-Elves on DNDBeyond

A blend of the long-lived Elves and the shorter, hastier humankind, Half-Elves bring traits of both. Half-Elves are as varied as humans and much of their heritage and culture is dependent on which Elven subrace they draw their ancestry from. They are shunned by neither of their parent races and fit well into most societies, their fine features and gentle natures often blending well with their human natural charisma. Each Half-Elf is different depending on which group of Elves and Humans they were born to, and they are as diverse as humans or Elves in that respect.

Half-Orcs on DNDBeyond

Despite the way most Orcs are regarded throughout the world of Aol, Half-Orcs mingle freely with others and face no distrust or discrimination from either side of their parentage in Miltern and Sogravia. Often stronger than your average humanoids, many half-orcs find themselves either in jobs of manual labour or physical strength, but strangely there are proportionately more Half-Orcs within the Temple Clergy than any other race; though most of them serve as temple guards and the like, they are still devout.

Halflings on DNDBeyond

It is rare to see a good inn without at least one halfling in its’ employ; masters of hospitality, welcome and hearth, the comforts of home are the goal of most halflings.There are halflings who prefer a simpler life of agriculture or travelling collecting tales, but most halflings are found in the inns, taverns, food stores and bakeries of the cities of Jazuc. Halflings are affable and cheerful people, they cherish the bonds of family and friendship as well as the comforts of hearth and home, harbouring few dreams of gold or glory.

Even the most adventurous among them usually venture into the world for reasons of community, friendship, wanderlust, or curiosity. They love discovering new things, even simple things, such as an exotic food or an unfamiliar style of clothing. Halflings make excellent merchants and negotiators and many have worked up the wealth to gain great importance within the cities they live in.

Harengon on DNDBeyond

Though many Harengon can live their whole lives without being seen by other civilised folk, these fun-loving mischievous tricksters have begun to find their way into the cities. Often extremely dextrous and acrobatic, they are often found employed as acrobats and entertainers or in more shady pursuits requiring the same skill sets. Harengons are notoriously full of energy, up for a challenge and fiercely intelligent. In the wilderness they continually practise their quick-thinking and agility in various games and friendly competitions. Harengons vary between 1 and 4 feet in height.

Hobgoblins on DNDBeyond

The Hobgoblins are a martial folk that made a bargain to protect the land of Sogravia in exchange for sheltering within its lands. Prior to the coming of the Shadowveil, the Hobgoblins were regarded as evil and enemies by most rational races, but these days their presence is tolerated, if not easily accepted. Hobgoblins are renowned for their martial prowess and tactical genuity, and many hobgoblin clans and families have been soldiers for countless generations.

Humans on DNDBeyond

The predominant race of the modern world, many an Elf has been heard to say that Humans are conquering the world merely with their breeding habits. Regardless, it is true that in the world of Jazuc there are more humans than any other peoples. As varied as the plant life or animal life of the world, little can be said to generalise humans except to note that they live their lives with great passion and haste; this may be due to the shortness of their lives. Humans are all over the world, and are as varied as any race can be.

Humans have been capable of the most heinous of evil crimes and the greatest of noble deeds; they are one of the few races that tolerate poverty, and allow members of their own race to starve and go homeless within their vast cities. Most Human cities are multicultural and diverse, as most humans are very open-minded and accepting of others, despite the way they isolate members of their own communities. Their cities are led by nobility and bloodline is often an important factor in the fate of a human. It can be impossible to judge the nature of any human by their race alone; they follow many Gods, many traditions and varied beliefs depending on where in the world they are born, their class, stature and wealth.

Inklings on DNDBeyond

Inklings are an amphibious race. They spend most of their time frolicking around the water, enjoying the beauty and bounty of the water. Other races may think them frivolous, but inklings know the value of a good time. Under the surface (literally and figuratively) inklings understand the depth and power of the water, and often do their best to emulate it.

Inkings can alternate between a humanoid form and a squid form. In either form, they produce natural ink that they spit from their mouths. In both forms, a healthy sheen of moisture covers their skin.

The humanoid form is slight and childlike, and has a black marking resembling a mask around their eyes, small round ears, and "hair" that resembles a squid's tentacles. These tentacles usually extend down the hips or lower back. Instead of individual teeth, they have two solid pieces of enamel similar to a squid's beak behind their lips.

Kalashtar on DNDBeyond

The Kalashtari’s origins are lost to time, but nowadays they travel freely across all of the world, for they are naturally explorers and wanderers at heart. Part of this is because of their nature, but for the most part this is because at the age of 20, when a Kalashtar comes of age, they go to seek out a place of natural affinity to renew their bond with the spirit that they are bound to from birth.

Kalashtari are bound to prime elemental spirits when they are first born; these can be as simple as Earth, Fire and Air spirits, or as complex as Decay, Grazeland, Desert and Waterfall spirits. Each of these spirits has its own nature, and though these spirits do not talk, they can communicate feelings. This can lead to Kalashtari taking on certain personality traits from their spirits. A Kalshatar bound to an Earth Spirit for example may be a patient and gentle person, slow to anger; quite the opposite may be true of one bound to a Fire Spirit.

Kalashtar tend to stop wandering when they are due to have a child, and settle down for twenty years or so, before continuing on their travels when their offspring makes their spirit journey to renew their affinity with their spirit. Just because parents have a certain nature of spiritual bond (perhaps they are both bound to water spirits) it does not mean that their children will also have a similar nature. Most Kalashtar find themselves adventuring across the land sooner or later as the wandering urges from their natural spirit companions press on them to do so. A few, however, settle in a place natural to the spirit they are bound with, and remain there for the rest of their years.

Kalashtar are usually a little taller than your average humanoid, usually between 5”8 and 7 feet tall. They tend to be thin and willowy in nature. They tend to live approximately 120-140 years, and when they die, someone must take the spirit that they were bound to to a place that is natural to them to lay it to rest so it may return to the wilds and bind once again to a Kalashtar in the future.

Kobolds on DNDBeyond

Kobolds tend to be gentle, wary and nervous creatures that rarely venture from the Underdark homes they live in to the surface. Most Kobolds spend their life inventing and creating, either selling their wares to other Underdark races or through intermediaries to the world above. Usually standing between 2 and 4 feet tall, the dextrous Kobolds have taken to artifice like a dragon to flame. With both a natural affinity for magic and quick minds, they are often found to be excellent inventors, creators and scientists.

These little beings maintain the belief that they are descendants of great dragons, and still worship the Great Dragons with ardour and passion. Kobolds can often trace the bloodlines of their tribes back many centuries and the small cultural differences between their coloration is mostly understood only by them.

Leonin on DNDBeyond

Much of the Leonin history is lost within Sogravia, as they were forced from their native lands of South Leigon long ago by the Shadowveil. As such, many Leonin in current-day Sogravia have found their places within the cities and armies of Sogravia. They are fearsome combatants by virtue of their size and natural speed and strength. Many Leonin believe in strength of arm, skill at the hunt and have an intensely complicated code of honour. Many regard the traditions of the Leonin as backwards and dated.

Elsewhere in the world, most Leonin are still fiercely tribal and there are still areas in which certain Prides reside and have done so for many years. Leonin are territorial, and it is customary to send word ahead of a visit. They distrust magic, and all things magical, and pay homage to various monuments within the islands. Water is a sacred resource to the Prides of the Leonin, and to fight near water is to incur the wrath of all Leonin and their Gods, or so it is believed. In tradition, water and Desert Oases are sacred and peaceful places where the Prides can gather to share tales and find partners.

Lizardfolk on DNDBeyond

The Lizardfolk are a race who seem to rush to complete as much as they can in their shorter lifespans than any other race. They are deeply ambitious, and apply this to whatever their trade is at the time. Most Lizardfolk see politics as a means to power and accomplishment, though Lizardfolk worldwide have been seen applying this ambition to all manners of trades and lifestyles.

They are a hardy folk, and thrive in varied environments, from jungle to deserts. Jungle Lizardfolk are renowned for the narcotics that they both grow and trade, the Yuan-ti are especially fond of these narcotics and in areas of jungle you can almost always find lizardfolk and Yuan-ti living side by side. The Lizardfolk are often quick to violence, much as they are quick to any other action within their lives. Most lizardfolk seem to try to sleep as little as possible, do as much as they can within their days, and repeat this the following day.

Loxodon on DNDBeyond

Rare is the Loxodon who leaves their tribe, though it has been known to have happened. Loxodon tribes are Matriarchal, and they tend to defer to their female compatriots at all times. Loxodon stand between 7 and 8 foot tall for the most part, are exceptionally heavy and live for 4 or 5 centuries.

They are a peaceful tribal folk who tend to avoid bigger cities and towns and keep to themselves. There are tribes all over the world and Loxodon place great importance upon their bloodlines and ancestry. Within Sogravia, most Loxodon have tended either towards heavy infantry roles in the army or to the study of magic within the Magus Arcane; despite their prowess in both areas, most Loxodon find themselves in isolation - never really fitting in with those around them. Few are the Loxodon who have adapted to being surrounded by people and noise.

Minotaurs on DNDBeyond

Minotaurs tend to be guards, soldiers and mercenaries, being naturally inclined towards the warriors’ arts. Minotaurs are very diverse in nature, some bearing large horns like a bull and some none at all. Most minotaurs revel in jewellery and treasure and have a primal desire to collect beauty.

Mousefolk on DNDBeyond

Mousefolk spend much of their days eating and sleeping. They tend to have extremely large families and value bloodlines and family over all else. The Mousefolk stand between 1 and 3 feet tall, and are divided into the Meadow folk and the Softpaws. Mousefolk tend to be resourceful and tenacious, and they believe in community over all else. The patterns of their fur dictate their hereditary history and family bloodlines and thus they keep incredibly good care of their fur. They are traders, craftsmen and hunters and are often incredibly skilled at remaining unseen.

Owlin on DNDBeyond

Most Owlin spend their lives in study and in contemplation, whether that study is of history, the arcane arts or the mystic crafts of their people. The Owlin worship the Goddess of the Night above all else, and almost all of them are nocturnal. The plumage of an Owlin develops over time with their age and magics, and a multi-coloured Owlin is assumably both aged and powerful in the ways of their magics. Owlin are often incredibly protective of their secrets and their homes and rarely venture to speak with the other races. Those Owlin found within Sogravia tend to study within the Magus Arcane and retain their reclusive nature.

Satyrs on DNDBeyond

Satyr traditions have not changed in many centuries, and as such Satyr can be divided into four groups by the God they worship. Those that worship Gaecilia are the gentle forest Satyr. Those that worship Jurus are those that tend towards the more mischievous and criminal lives, though there are also many clerics of Jurus who are not criminal at all. There are those that worship Borlean and study the arcane arts, or become tradesfolk of the more complex type, such as apothecaries and alchemists. Most City Satyr worship Borlean.

Lastly, there are those that worship Quan. The Quan-worshipping Satyr are the nomadic, fun-loving, all singing, all dancing Satyrs for which the race is most well known. These Satyr can be found in towns and cities, performing on the road and in taverns and generally worshipping life, love and laughter.

Shifters on DNDBeyond

A fierce folk, excellent in battle and well known for their fearsome beastly transformations. Despite this, however, outside of battle the shifters are mostly a peaceful folk who worship the land and their totem animals. Shifters are often on the edge of wildness and can be a little chaotic however. The Shifters believe in being self-reliant and conjuring inner strength in times of need rather than relying on someone else. A common saying for a shifter is “preparing for the journey yet to come” which reflects how they believe the world changes constantly and its important to be prepared for whatever comes.

Shifters believe that the reward for this independence is freedom, and will always feel uncomfortable around strict authority for these reasons; Shifters often don’t meld well into settlements with complex law structures. Shifters often change moods in swift and dramatic fashion and find it difficult to control their emotions in stressful situations. Shifters often take on personality traits of their totem animals without meaning to; it is not uncommon to see a shifter snarl or growl.

Tabaxi on DNDBeyond

The Tabaxi of Eldessa and Miltern are rarely found outside of their old ancestral villages. With a complex hierarchy based on Caste and age and an unwavering sense of duty, the Matriarchal people hearken back to a much older time. Whilst plenty from the outside might consider the practices of the Tabaxi cruel, outdated and barbaric, few would say so in earshot of the stern and uncompromising race. In Sogravia, the Tabaxi were forced into close quarters with the many other civilised races, and as such have become a little more relaxed in their ways. Nevertheless within their homes, the Tabaxi remain a very traditional people.

Thri-Kreen on DNDBeyond

Thri-kreen resemble bipedal praying mantises. Their bodies are coated in exoskeletons with various earthy hues, including rust red, sandy yellow, and mottled green-brown. Unlike most humanoids, thri-kreen possess six limbs, walking on two legs and possessing four arms, each ending in four prehensile, claw-like fingers. The lower two arms are smaller and less-powerful than the upper two.

Their heads possess compound eyes, generally in somewhat-brighter colours like green or blue, antennae, and mandibles. Because of the alien shape of their jaws, they have difficulty speaking and pronouncing Common and most other humanoid languages besides their own. Thri-Kreen and Aaba are often found together and both are well adapted to survive in the untamed wildernesses of the world.

Tortles on DNDBeyond

The Tortles are a very solitary race, maturing within a few years of their birth and setting out travelling around the world. Tortles are not a very populous race, but its not unusual to find one wandering through the cities and wilderness anywhere in the world. They are a long-lived race, often living up to a millenia in age, and they tend to be slow to conflict, instead preferring to ponder both sides of any given argument. There are almost no Tortles in positions of responsibility, most instead preferring to remain as observers within the world. Tortles tend towards the more magical and divine arts rather than defending themselves with martial abilities. What few Tortles are found in Sogravia tend towards priesthood or study in the Magus Arcane.

Triton on DNDBeyond

The Tritons are a fierce race of individuals well adapted to aquatic environments. They are a matriarchal society who have deep religious beliefs that they follow faithfully and ardently. Their underwater cities are incredibly beautiful to any who are able to visit, though they rarely ever let outsiders visit.

A proud people with many preconceptions as a result of their way of life who believe the world owes them due to the evils under the sea that this race single-handedly keep at bay. Perhaps they are right. Very few Tritons are found on land, and those who dwell on land are often exiles of their own cities.

Vedalken on DNDBeyond

A race who are never content with where they are, but instead seek endless improvement and a perfection that cannot be achieved, the Vedalken are fiercely intelligent. They mature slowly and age very slowly, often living many centuries. They place intellectual prowess and understanding above all else, and this is reflected by the Gods they worship. Vedalken are often moved towards contemplative and scholarly lifestyles within the temple or studying the Arts; this is made all the more natural by the powerful magics that often flow through their veins.

Warforged on DNDBeyond

In Sogravia, the practice of Warforging began as a means to shut out the Shadowveil influence, originally created from binding rituals bringing wizardly magic into Dwarven-forged metal. Despite being a somewhat barbaric ritual that can be incredibly painful for the person experiencing it, Warforging has been made popular by the rich and aristocratic, mostly to ensure their servants and bodyguards cannot be infiltrated by the Shadowveil. The Warforged retain very few of their memories before their forging, and are often incapable of feeling some levels of empathy and emotion due to this.

Yuan-ti on DNDBeyond

The Yuan-ti are cold blooded in more ways than one. Found on the edges of jungles and swamps where they can grow narcotics and pleasure drugs, the yuan-ti are a viciously competent group. The Yuan-ti are often pirate captains, drug traffickers and shady members of the criminal underworld. Often addicted to various narcotics grown within the jungles surrounding them, as many of them use anti-toxins to avoid being poisoned as pleasure drugs, it is rare to find a yuan-ti with a clear mind unless he is in full need of his wits.

Whilst some Yuan-ti do find their way clear of both the drugs so commonly related to them and the criminal upbringings they often grow up in, they are unfortunately few and far between. Yuan-ti in Sogravia are highly valued for their alchemical knowledge and aid with creating poisons that affect the Shadowveil; even so, few Yuan-ti have done much to endear themselves to the regular populace.

The world of Ael uses the same Pantheons as Forgotten Realms; details of every Deity can be found here. Some of the most important gods within that Pantheon are listed below. The one exception to this is the Raven Queen, who is very modified and different in the world of Ael.

Lathander on the Forgotten Realms Wiki

Lathander, often referred to as the Morninglord, is often symbolized as a Rising Sun and is a key diety for the Dawnguard as well as one of the most worshipped Gods within Sogravia. Lathander is the God of the Dawn, Birth, Renewal, Life, Spring, Youth, Vitality and Healing.

Lathander is often depicted as a giant of a man to match the size of his lover, Chauntea, and is known to be a kind and gentle God who is always optimistic and generous of spirit. Clerics of Lathander are known as Dawnbringers or Priests/Priestesses of the Dawn depending on their specializations.

Mystra on the Forgotten Realms Wiki

Known as the Mistress of the Weave, or Mother of all Magic, Mystra is the Goddess of Magic. She is eternally focused on the balance of magic within the world and pays little attention to the whim and worry of mortals.

Mystra is known to be a shapechanger and often appears in different guises depending on her purpose. She is not concerned with ethics such as good or evil, but to try and defy the laws of magic is to invite one's death at Mystra's hands. Clerics of Mystra are known as Clerics of the Weave, and incredibly powerful wizards or sorcerers are often referred to as the Chosen of Mystra.

Oghma on the Forgotten Realms Wiki

Known as the Binder, or Lord of Knowledge, Oghma is the god of Learning, Invention, Inspiration and Knowledge. Oghma is known to hold anything of beauty or creativity in high esteem, but is in fact the God of all knowledge and thought.

Often depicted as a genial and jovial man, Oghma is often tohught of as cheerful and carefree, despite his legendary wisdom. Clerics of Oghma are known as Acolytes of Knowledge, Acolytes of Learning and Oghman Scribes.

Mielikki on the Forgotten Realms Wiki

Mielikki is known as the Lady of the Forest and is the Goddess of Nature, the Forest, and the Woodlands. She is a gentle Goddess who pays as close attention to the small animals and plants of the woodlands as she does the mortals that dwell within those lands.

Mielikki is often depicted as a youthful woman of vitality and grace, and she is often shown as cheerful, joyous and peaceful. Chosen of Mielikki are usually powerful Druids or Rangers and Clerics of Mielikki are simply known as Druids or Rangers of Mielikki.

Lolth on the Forgotten Realms Wiki

Lolth is the Queen of Spiders, Lady of Chaos and Goddess of the Drow. She is as at home in the Abyss as she is in the immortal realms and she revels in cruelty, torture and chaos. Most of her following are drow, but lesser demons and beings of chaos also look to her dark calling.

Usually depicted as a beautiful drow or a spider from the waist down, Lolth is nothing but cruel, dark and evil. Followers of Lolth are usually Priestesses of Lolth, though there are some Clerics of Lolth who are male.

Tyr on the Forgotten Realms Wiki

Tyr is often referred to as the God of Justice, Blind Overlord or the One-Handed. Tyr is the God of bravery, self-sacrifice, justice and law. Often depicted as blind or maimed, Tyr is known to be strong-spirited, noble and brave; Though he could be stern and rigid. Clerics of Tyr are known as Lawbringers, Clerics of the Just or simply Priests/Priestesses of Tyr.

Silvanus on the Forgotten Realms Wiki

Silvanus, the Forest Father or Old Oak is the God of the Wilderness, Wild Nature and Flora. Silvanus is unpredictable and is often far more concerned with the natural order and wilderness than with any followers. Maintaining a balance between growth and decay he is often seen as savage and unyielding. Followers of Silvanus are usually referred to as Druids of the Wild; rare are the followers of Silvanus who are not Druids.

Chauntea on the Forgotten Realms Wiki

Chauntea is the Goddess of Life, Bounty and Agriculture. Often referred to as the Earthmother, Great Mother or Mother of all Flowers, Chauntea is nurturing and gentle in her ways. Chauntea is as much the goddess of cultured flowers, crops and agriculture as she is the natural world and everything in it.

One of the most popular of the Deities of Ael, Chauntea is worshipped by a large percentage of the commonfolk for her healing, gift of life and cultivation of agriculture. Chauntea is often depicted as a giant beautiful human woman with long shiny hair braided around her head. Clerics of Chauntea are known as the Order of the Earthmother, Clerics of the Earthmother or Daughters of Chauntea.

Moradin on the Forgotten Realms Wiki

Father of the Dwarves, the Soul Forger Moradin is the chief diety of the Dwarves. Known to be a harsh but fair god who embodies strength, justice and force of will, all Dwarves worship Moradin in some aspect of their life. Depicted as a great forgemaster, Moradin is held in the greatest of regards by every Dwarf.

Corellon on the Forgotten Realms Wiki

More fully known as Corellon Larethian, the Father of Elves is known as the Protector, Father of Elveinkind and Preserver of Life. Seen as the personification of all things Elven, Corellon is both Warrior and Poet, Bard and Archer, Male and Female. Usually depicted as male, Corellon is both arrogant and prideful yet gentle and forgiving. All Elves pay at least some tribute to Corellon in their worship of the Gods.

Bahamut on the Forgotten Realms Wiki

The Draconic God of Justice, Bahamut is known as the Angel of the Seven, the God of Dragons and Justicemaker. His natural form is that of a platinum dragon and he is said to be the only one of his kind. Primarily the deity of metallic dragons, worship of Bahamut has also been replicated in all Dragonborn, who percieve him as the father of justice, and the God of all Dragonborn.

Cyrrollalee on the Forgotten Realms Wiki

Cyrrollalee is the most popular of Halfling Deities, known as the Goddess of Trust, Friendship and Hospitality, all of which the Halflings regard as matters of the utmost importance. Also referred to as the Hand of Fellowship or the Hearthkeeper, Cyrrollalee is a gentle and kind Goddess who welcomes all to her fold. Clerics of Cyrrollalee are referred to as Stewards/Stewardesses of the Hearth.

Flandal Steelskin on the Forgotten Realms Wiki

Flandal Steelskin is the Gnommish God of Mining and Smithing often referred to as the Great Steelsmith, Master of Metal or simply the Armorer. In modern Sogravia, many gnomes have become artificers and blacksmiths, and as such worship of Flandal Steelskin is incredibly popular. He is a gentle god who believes in achieving ones' goals through determination and creativity. Clerics of Flandal are known as the Hammers.

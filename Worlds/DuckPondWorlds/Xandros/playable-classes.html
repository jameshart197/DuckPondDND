Playable Classes in Xandros

For the purpose of displaying an alternate world and the changes from the original world-state, certain homebrew sub-classes have been added to the world. Many of these are specific to a certain city, race or group of people. Details are given for each one below.

The Order of the Ancient Weapon Masters are a guard as old as Arrenus itself. When the Bastion was first formed in the old Amphitheatre of Gladiators, the gladiators themselves took to defending the populace from the monsters that attempted to get in. In honour of their valiant sacrifices and their efforts to defend the people, the Order of Ancient Weapon Masters is still retained to this day.

They train with martial weapons as opposed to those made with technology, and wield them more skillfully than any others in the land. Although members of any race can join the Order, the majority of the members of the Order are Dragonborn, just as the old Gladiators were.

The art of the Arcane Gunsmith is at once both simple and dangerous. Wielding hand held thundercannons, these rock gnomes and mountain dwarves from Mechania are fearsome foes on the battlefield. Maintaining these weapons alone takes training and understanding, let alone operating them. Members of the 7th Battalion are the only people with understanding of the making and use of these guns, and their blueprints are guarded closely.

The Githyanki Arcane Knights are a fearsome combination of magic and blade. Mostly, these warriors are found defending the Enclave alongside their Animated Armor units, though a few are found elsewhere in the world. They are master tacticians and students of battle from both old and new times.

Arcane Polymaths are the result of combining near unrivalled intelligence, a hive mind and a requirement for magic arcane. Mind Flayers are capable of learning spells of all schools with the same detachment that they have studied in all manner of subjects over the years. Maintaining a varied arsenal of spells both psionic and arcane, the Arcane Polymaths of Academia are renowned throughout Xandros.

Primarily members of the Enclave, Archivists are the Enclaves arms, with an incredibly long reach. Travelling throughout the world to gather accounts of information pre and post sundering, most Archivists have a specialty. Some focus on the new technologies being created, some on pre-Sundering wildlife, but each will travel relentlessly to learn all they can.

The Blood Mages of Newton were once the only thing that stood between the Bastion and the mad monsters that hounded them. To this day the Order are revered within Newton and entrusted with the city's defences. In more modern times, the arts of the Blood Mages have been taught to others, but it was once an act of sheer desperation by a group of survivors desperate to cling to life that created this ancient order. Any race can be a member of the Blood Arcana, but most of them tend to be human.

When the Bastion upon Lazros was built, there was a group of monks from a nearby monastery that trained relentlessly to mimic the arts of monsters and beasts to enhance their abilities. When the Bastion was threatened, these monks used their training and combined with the mages from the monastery in an attempt to defend the populace. The art of the Blue Mage was born in Lazros, and to this day there are very few who study its traditions from outside the city. Entrance to the Monastery of the Blue Mages is incredibly exclusive, and just entering the Monastery as an Acolyte by no means guarantees graduation. Most Blue Mages are Shifters.

Many adapted to survive the Sundering, but few as much as the Druids had to. The very nature of nature was twisted during the Sundering, and many Druids who were left upon the Earth had to change their ways and understanding drastically to incorporate the new world around them. Most Druids ended up mimicking nature in their every action and decision, channelling the rules of survival of the fittest to cope with their new environments.

Druids that, after the Sundering, specialised in trying to maintain the flora and fauna of the old world ended up as Circle of Evolution Druids. In present times, these Druids are the brave wanderers who travel the wilderness to seek new life, be it animal or plant, and care for it. Corpse Orchids are plants that have gained sentience and often twist their growths to create companions for themselves by awakening plants or taking over beasts as their hosts; their companions are double the CR of other races playing this class.

Some Druids who had given up on venturing into the Wilderness and seeing nature first hand instead turned their hands to the weather and to controlling Airships, resulting in a specialisation of Air Druids. One of the advantages for these pilots is the ability to fly themselves upon the air currents, so even if the ship goes down, the pilot will likely survive the drop.

Students of Hymns of the Old Age, bards who weave divine magic into their songs are known as College of Hymns Bards. Through combining traditional bardic techniques with holy guidance, these bards are healers, priests and protectors. Originating in the Lycan city of Lunethra, the Songs of Selune in the temples there, sung by thousands of wolves calling out for their Goddess, is a sight to be witnessed.

Clerics of Fate use divine magic to nudge events to their liking, embodying the power of chance and its manipulation, and range from whimsical meddlers to wise prophets. Masters of destiny, they pluck the threads of fate like a puppeteer, bringing fortune to themselves and their allies, or misfortune to their foes. These Clerics rarely pray to a specific deity, instead directing their efforts at Fate itself, though some are Patrons of Tymora, remembering her from the Pantheon of Old.

Gadgeteers are the masters of setting up and disarming traps, finding clever solutions that others would not and always having the right tool on hand. These clever mechanics are a vital requirement on an airship that can’t afford an Android mechanic. Capable of crafting unusual and helpful items as well as upgrading their weapons in various ways, the Gadgeteer is always a useful partner to have around. Gadgeteers are usually from Mechania, and are most often Rock Gnomes or Androids. Those that choose the Android, Rock Gnome or Dwarf race and a background from Mechania will gain access to the Mending and Greater Mending cantrips for free and may create Clockwork Toys, Fire Starts, Music Boxes and Flashlights.

Studying without restrictions or guidance in the dangerous Scavenger Towns, Hedge Wizards contort the rules of classic magic. Able to bind to unusual familiars, contort their bodies into the shape of small beasts and connect directly to the planes of the elements, these wizards may be unschooled, but they do not lack for power.

In the city of Kreegaa and the surrounding lands, the Aaba shamans are known for their wisdom and their power. Summoners of great power, at home with the nature around them, despite its’ unusual contortions, these summoners call creatures to their aid for menial tasks or for defence. Whilst others in the world may be Master Summoners, only the Aaba can summon more dangerous creatures. Aaba who take this class may increase the CR availability within any summon spell by 1.

There are two iterations of the Master at Arms within the world of Xandros. One is the group of battle tacticians trained by the Enclave to lead their Units. The other are the Thri-Kreen battalions within the Kreegaa army. Both of these soldiers train relentlessly with old world weapons, combining both hands into a skilful fusion of precision and brutality.

The Clerics of Lunethra are powerful and devoted followers of Selune, dedicated to returning her to power. To this end, they have a deep connection with the Twin Moons ever-present in the Xandros sky, despite the thick cloud cover. These Clerics maintain a passion for their calling throughout their lives, whilst practising their craft with the wisdom of the Elders.

To be Unburdened, Unyielding, Unconquered, Unmuted and Unhesitating is the calling of the Oath of Storms Paladin. A Paladin who has made a pact with the ever crackling lightning of the thick clouds of Xandros, and learnt to channel it not only in the flying of their beloved Airships, but into battle. These Paladins swear by Talos, despite his fall from the Pantheon, and they are a veritable tempest of justice and virtue.

The Nightwalkers are an Order unto their own. The Order of the Vampire are based within the City of Evernight and are their most elite guards. Mostly composed of predator races such as Leonin and Aarakocra, these already strong mortals take on a level of immortality by embracing the vampiric gifts and transferring vampiric blood into their own bodies through dark rituals. These Nightwalkers are dedicated and vicious warriors.

When there are guns, there are those who would use them to their own ends. The Outlaws are those that make their own way in the world, oftentimes taking what they need when they need it. They are the assassins you pay to rid you of a problem, the thieves who take the treasure you were seeking or the Mercenaries who, for the right pay, are willing to do whatever it takes to achieve their employers’ goals.

Fearsome are the Animated Armor Juggernauts that the Enclave create to defend their lands. Taught the ways of combat by the talented Githyanki Arcane Knights, these brutes use their heavy armor and the sheer force of their body to decimate their foes and protect their creators. With deadly force hammers attached to each arm, these Juggernauts are an unstoppable force.

Revered within the Goliath communities, the Skalds are part storyteller, part musician, part warrior. They often are found upon the battlefield amongst their comrades, inspiring them to greater deeds and riling them to higher levels of bloodlust. They are fearsome warriors within their own right too; the greatest tales of battle are those witnessed from the battlefield itself. Much of the Goliath community can be found within the walls of Lunethra, keeping to their ancient traditions and eschewing most technology.

Sworn never to harm another, Preservation Clerics seek to maintain all elements of life and history. They are historians as well as priests, teaching the ways of Oghma and seeking to learn as much as they can of the world, past and present. These priests are usually heavily armoured with great tower shields they use to protect those from harm who are at risk. They are often found travelling with Archivists throughout the world of Xandros, protecting them and learning alongside them.

The Elder Vampires within the City of Evernight are a force to be reckoned with. Practised in the arts of melee combat and arcane sorcery, they are fast, ruthless and deadly. These vampires have progressed beyond fearing sunlight or running water. For the most part, Pureblood Vampires belong to the clan of Maison de la Nuit, though some other ancient clans still remain throughout Xandros. Although fearsome combatants, you are far more likely to witness a Pureblood in the field of politics than battle.

Witches are learned spellcasters like wizards, but unlike traditional wizards, they gain their magic not in schools but at the fringes of society, learning from the shunned and adopting folklore and old ways that frighten common folk but offer great power. They bind pieces of their souls to items and creatures they create, making them lightning rods for the energies of the universe. It is said that the magic witches use is descended from the oldest practices of magic in the multiverse. Most Witches belong to the Coven of Shared Truth, believing that knowledge of the Old ways should be shared for mutual benefit. Many minor witches throughout Xandros find their way to the Coven sooner or later.

Transport in Xandros

Airship

The most common form of travel in the Wilderness is by Airship, though they are expensive to build and complex to pilot. Building an Airship requires knowledge of the art of Magnetic Plating, which absorbs power from the unnatural weathers of Xandros and channels it into a ring that propels the Airship. Piloting an Airship requires the ability to manipulate and harness the weather to adjust the velocity and direction of the Airship; Experienced pilots will also be able to manage an Airship through more dangerous weathers, or control a larger vehicle without too much assistance.

Airships were first created by the Rock Gnomes of Mechania, though now many different people have learnt the art of creating them and put their own flair on their creations. Nevertheless, they all use the same basic premise, although whereas those created by the Rock Gnomes tend to generate flame to propel the ship, variants that use lightning have been seen more recently, as there is less likelihood of a fire.

Airship Pilots are known as Tempestarii. Most of these tend to be storm sorcerers, druids specialising in the air and weather, paladins who make an oath with the storms and wizards who have studied their whole lives to control the airstreams.

Canal Boats

Canal Boats are usually powered by burning various whatever they have on hand and are often noisy, smelly and slow. Giant wheels turn to propel them through the water, and travel via canal boat between cities can take days, but they’re cheaper and easier to build than airships by a sizable margin, and much, much easier to captain. Simple and quick for both repair and maintenance, Canal Boats remain a very popular option.

Pods

Pods are incredibly expensive. They are like bikes or cars with a single wheel instead of two that you sit inside. Used mostly by couriers, the ultra-rich, and the relay-runners in Mechania. Somewhat uncomfortable and noisy, this is a trade-off for their impressive speed. Known for being dangerous especially on treacherous terrain. Mcgristle was famous for taking one once out into the wilderness. Unsurprisingly, he didn’t return. Pods are powered by crushed rubies that are enchanted to smoulder, added to distilled coal tar and used to fuel the steam engine which powers them. They are really slow off the mark, accelerate like a slug, but once up to speed their speed can rival even airships. Hard to stop with weak brakes, the level of danger involved with driving these vehicles cannot be understated. Unsurprisingly, this is a popular sport in Arrenus, and an impressive racetrack weaves through the city specifically for this.

Railways

For a long time the only railway in the world was in Mechania, which travels from the bottom to the top of the mountain. This railway uses captured lightning elementals to power the rails, and the train then travels upon these rails. Different, however, is the much later creation of the steam railway that travels from Morena to the City of Evernight. Built by the manpower of undead slaves, these rails carry a single train called Nightpiercer. A train that uses a combination of steam and necromantic energy to travel along the rails at frightening speeds, delivering goods to and from Morena to the Vampire Lords of Evernight.

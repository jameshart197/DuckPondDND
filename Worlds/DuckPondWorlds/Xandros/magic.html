Magic in Xandros

Magic is a tool that has become a necessity in the world of Xandros. Whether it is for the purpose of summoning food and drink, keeping the weather away from cities or imbuing the latest technologies with the essences required to maintain them, magic has become a natural part of every day life. Most citizens of Xandros can at least call upon minor magics to assist them, such as the ability to produce light or remove ash from their clothing.

Powerful mages are usually incredibly rich, paid vast sums of money to maintain important parts of the city, create food and drink for the rich and wealthy or imbue magic into the newest of inventions and creations. Despite this, there are very few powerful mages within the world. Instead, there is a vast amount of mediocre mages, making their living by doing these tasks on a much smaller level.

Most of the more powerful magic users throughout the world are sorcerers, not wizards, who are powerful by nature not practice. In the world of Xandros, life is dangerous and fast; studying for long periods of time may not be possible unless you start life with an advantage. The most notable wizards in the world are likely the Mind Flayer Polymaths or the Blue Mages of Lazros. The most notable sorcerers are the Forbidden and the Elder Vampire Purebloods of Evernight.

There are also powerful priests to be found throughout the world of Xandros; less Gods does not necessarily mean less Divine magic; only a change of source, or a smaller focus. Preservation Clerics from the Enclave boast powerful defensive magics, and the Clerics of Death and the Grave in Morena keep vast depths of knowledge from ancient times in their temples.

Despite this, the majority of those with arcane talent are found assisting the artificers, builders and inventors throughout the world of Xandros. Using simple cantrips and repetitive rituals, they are the mages who allow the world to function as it does today. Giving small magics to minor technological instruments, these are the magic users who give the world of Xandros enough food to eat, shelter to live beneath, light to see with, water to drink and turn the cities to the safe havens they are today. They enable transport and trade, communication and protection and all of the small luxuries the rich and powerful take for granted.

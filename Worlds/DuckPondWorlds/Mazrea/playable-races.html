The Races of Mazrea

Whilst there are orcs, goblinkind, drow, duergar and some other races in the world who are most definitely civilized races, they are also inherently evil. As well list the werefolk, demons and other evil beings that dwell in Mazrea. As such, only races of mostly goodly weal, or at least those that dwell in the cities of traditional civilization, have been listed here.

Aarakocra on DNDBeyond

Aarakocra are spread throughout the world of Mazrea, though they are most commonly found in the Eyries of the Northlands and Kelaan. Aarakocra tend to be insular and isolationist, and are rarely interested in taking positions of authority outside of their homelands. Even within their communities, Aarakocra tend to be solitary in nature, often ranging far and wide alone. Most Aarakocra worship Aquila, a member of the Constellation.

Aasimar on DNDBeyond

Once thought to be the descendants of celestials, Aasimar are renowned for their beauty and their ability to manifest wings. These days, however, most Aasimar are known to be fierce traders of old bloodlines and often good wealth. The Aasimar often find themselves as highly prized courtesans, concubines and husbands or wives of the rich when their bloodlines do not grant them automatic access to the nobility. They can often be arrogant and self-righteous, and are very firm in their beliefs. Aasimar are often devoutly religious, unwavering in their faith and doctrine.

Various Aasimar are found ruling throughout the Archipelago. Significant Aasimar populations can be found in South-Western Gallania and North-Western Kelaan. They often make powerful Sorcerers and Clerics, and no matter the path in life they choose, they’re ambitious, determined, and relentless in their pursuit for power and knowledge.

Bugbears on DNDBeyond

Bugbears are an unassuming, peaceful race for the most part with roots in the Feywild. Often preferring lives as travelling alchemists, huntsmen and the like, many bugbears prefer a solitary lifestyle. Nonetheless, due to their stature and natural martial abilities, it is not rare to find bugbears as soldiers and guards in the employ of the rich and wealthy. There are many Bugbears throughout Kelaan, though they often prefer solitary roles within small villages.

Centaurs on DNDBeyond

The Centaur Herds roam the hills and plains of Gallania, always on the move. They go to the Hunt weekly and all centaurs that are able participate as the Herd hunts Elk, Wyverns, anything that is swift and a challenge. The Rite of the Hunt is sacred to them, and even Centaurs that venture elsewhere in the world will often feel the call to the Hunt. The Centaur are a deeply reflective folk, and they worship the stars and constellations. Most Centaurs are deeply prideful and will brook no insults to themselves or their people, calling any such person who would throw insults to the Draw, a duel sacred in the Centaur culture.

Centaurs are very tribal and tend to dismiss other races as insignificant, their traditions bind them, but also keep them a little rural in comparison to many other races; Centaurs live on the move, and rarely stop in cities or towns, they trade little and they live a nomadic life. They tend to be very strong of arm, and have little tolerance for the old, weak or sickly. Centaurs come of age as young as 12, and live for many centuries.

Changelings on DNDBeyond

Changelings are often regarded with distrust and fear due to their abilities to emulate others, nevertheless, they are often the most well paid of spies, assassins and thieves. Due to their unique abilities, changelings often found themselves desired either for criminal activity or as concubines or courtesans; those who do manage to find a life outside of these constraints often do so as actors, entertainers or by remaining in one persona for their entire public life. It is impossible to place an estimate on Changeling numbers, due to their nature, though there are many living throughout the Archipelago and Kelaan. Few Changelings take positions of power, though they are often found high up in the hierarchies of those in power.

Dragonborn on DNDBeyond

The Dragonborn in the world of Mazrea are a diverse and spread out folk. Primarily worshipping the three Dragon Gods and the souls of Dragons past, these creatures came into being from prolonged proximity to Dragons over the ages. They tend to resemble the Dragons that made them both in terms of their dispositions and their features. The Golden Dragonborns were the first, as Golden Dragons tend to be the most gentle and benevolent. Golden Dragonborns are the Ruling Caste of the Dragonborn culture worldwide, and all Golden Dragonborns worldwide will be greatly respected and are the very top of the Dragonborn Hierarchy. The Silver Dragonborns came second and have always acted as Shield-Brothers to their Golden Kin, their right-hand-men so to speak.

Bronze Dragonborn have always been the caretakers of Dragons and are respected by both chromatic and metallic dragonborn for the work they have done. They have created cities within the great deserts of Gallania that Dragons can walk freely within and feel at home in, they are known for their Dragonsingers, bards of great renown who sing the praises and beauties of Dragons, appeasing the vain creatures. They are also known to be expert Dragon Groomers.

Brass and Copper Dragonborn have at various times held different roles within the Dragonborn community, be that the roles of assisting in caring for Dragons, or latter as expert craftsmen and tradespeople; Brass and Copper Dragonborns have always been the “middle class” of Dragonborn Culture.

Traditionally, Chromatic Dragons were evil; though many have since been quelled by their metallic kin, or at least kept somewhat under control. The same, however, is not true for the Chromatic Dragonborn that they once created. Historically, Chromatic Dragonborn were the Dragon-Riders, who rode upon dragons creating havoc and terror wherever they went. That is, however, ancient history. Nowadays the Chromatic Dragons are the lesser caste; they are mostly the serfs, farmers, servants and lesserfolk of the Dragonborn culture, however there are some exceptions.

Black Dragonborn are typically the most magically gifted of all Dragonborn, and those who are tend to be quite well regarded within the Dragonborn communities. Red Dragonborn tend to be the best of craftsmen when it comes to Blacksmithing and Metal-Working. White and Blue Dragonborn tend to unanimously be slaves, serfs, servants and smallfolk. Green Dragonborn are almost all slaves or criminals.

Dryads on DNDBeyond

The Dryads can be found in forests all over the world of Mazrea. They tend to keep to the quieter and more mysterious and magical forests, such as the Glurvyndale. The Dryads tend to keep themselves to themselves and only really the Elves get to see this elusive race on a regular basis. Living within trees and bound to a tree spirit themselves, rare is it that a Dryad would venture from the forest. However, it does happen on occasion; there was one traveling dryad who carried a small sapling of her soul tree with her as she traveled the world teaching of the strange herbs and plants she had discovered and of alchemical wonders.

The Dryads tend to be very protective of each other, and outside of mating season will rarely be seen. During mating season however, when the Dryads are looking for partners, they can be incredibly enthusiastic when male visitors come to their forests. A Dryad in the midsummer’s heat can be positively dangerous!

Dwarves on DNDBeyond

Dwarves are a very clannish group, and although they are scattered throughout Mazrea, they are always loyal to one another. There are dwarfhomes throughout Beldania and Kelaan, and they seem to have a way of remaining in contact with each other. The original dwarfhome, or at least, the seat of Dwarven Royalty is Ellodar in Beldania. King Igor Ironbeard currently holds the throne in Ellodar; almost all dwarves will, at some point in their lives, make the trip to Ellodar to see the true home of the Dwarves.

Dwarves tend to be a solitary group, not particularly welcoming of outsiders, though never truly turning anyone in need from their doorsteps. They are notoriously racist however, and will always value the lives of Dwarves above any others. They tend to stick to their large underground cities, though some dwarves have chosen to venture forth from these citadels to visit the wider world. One notable Dwarven Clan is the Greenbeards, who live above ground in the forests of Weylynd, where they all dye their beards green and worship the forest gods.

Eladrin on DNDBeyond

Eladrin are beautiful and deeply magical Elves that tend to reside in places of incredible natural beauty. Very careful in their avoidance of cities, most are sorcerers and wizards who keep to themselves and have their own business to mind, or small families travelling quietly within ancient forests and glades. When Eladrin do interact with others in the world, it is often in places of worship or study, such as the Academy of Shar, the University of Vazra and various temples.

Elves on DNDBeyond

The Elves are split into three categories, the Sun Elves, the High Elves and the Wood Elves;

Sun Elf

The Sun Elves are typical forest-based Elves who live in their own cities within the trees. What delineates them from the Wood Elves is the fact they can draw magic from the forests themselves, both to protect them and to protect their people. Fool is the man who attacks a Sun Elf in their own dwelling. Sun Elves tend to be distrustful of those outside their borders, though they are always willing to help those in need. They dwell mostly within the Glurvyndale, though there are a fair few in Vuralian forests as well.

High Elf (City Elf)

The High Elves are aristocratic elves that have moved out of the forests and into the cities. They are often the high nobility within a city, though a second strand of these High Elves prefer the simplistic life of village folk and have moved out to the smaller villages to pursue lives where creativity and beauty rule; these people are often tradesfolk such as potters and painters. The City Elves, as the High Elves have come to be known, tend more to the noble lifestyles, throwing lavish balls, playing the games of intrigue and romance at court, and vying to be seen as important.

Wood Elf

The Wood Elves describe themselves as those closest to the earth of the Elven Clans. The Wood Elves live exclusively within the forests of Vuralia and the Glurvyndale, and will rarely venture from those lands. They are the protectors of those woods, and they live in harmony with all of the nature within the woods. The Wood Elves tend to be exceptional archers, deadly with a sword, and exquisitely beautiful. They spend a lot of their time singing and dancing within the forests, and live a generally carefree life.

Fairies on DNDBeyond

The Fairies tend to live in places of natural beauty, be these waterfalls, lakes, forests or otherworldly caverns. These small creatures tend to be solitary from the age of 9 or 10. They mature quickly although never truly maturing, and tend to be very nature based. Some small few have entered cities and the aristocracy but they are few and far between. Usually a playful group that worship gods of laughter, joy, natural beauty and mischief, they are a gentle folk. They have no singular place of origin and tend not to have larger communities but instead smaller families dotted around. Most Fairies have inherent magic that they use to protect themselves despite their smaller stature, as most fairies stand between 3 and 4 feet tall. The nature of this magic, however, is usually the ability to hide and stay unnoticed when the larger folk come by.

Firbolgs on DNDBeyond

The Firbolgs are the gentle giants of the world. They tend to live at the foothills of mountains, and around the edges of forests, and are often regarded as protectors of the land itself. They tend towards the druidic arts and can almost always be found alone or in simple families of three. Firbolgs live longer lives than most humanoid races, and tend to live most of those lives in the same place; therefore if you find a Firbolg in his natural homeland, he will always be the most knowledgeable person about the local land. Firbolgs have a natural racial inclination to distrust Goliaths, as in their mythology, their Gods are continually at war. Firbolgs are not a warlike folk however, and are for the most part gentle and simple beings. Firbolg rarely mate for life, instead coming together to create offspring and raise it, and then moving apart again, as their first love is always for the land in which they live.

Firenewts on DNDBeyond

Firenewts are a tribal folk that rose from the Throat of Nessus in the Archipelago approximately a century ago. There was a violent and tumultuous disagreement between two factions of the Firenewts, one faction remaining in the Ebony Peaks, and the other travelling until they found their homes in the fiery deserts of the Savage Lands, in the far North West of Gallania. Firenewts are often savage, aggressive and quick to violence and as such, they remain within their small tribes. Whilst it is uncommon, some few travellers have visited the Firenewts peacefully, and tell tales of their strong pyromancers and deep connection with the land.

Flumphs on DNDBeyond

Flumphs communicate telepathically. Though they resemble jellyfish, flumphs are sentient beings of great intelligence and wisdom, possessing advanced knowledge of religion, philosophy, mathematics, and countless other subjects. Flumphs are sensitive to the emotional states of nearby creatures. If a creature's thoughts suggest goodness, a flumph seeks that creature out. When facing creatures that exude evil, a flumph flees.

Flumphs are often foundin the Underdark, and those who do live above land tend towards small, squat homes that allow in little light. Flumphs are often found at study in the University of Vazra or the Academy of Shar, and many Flumphs are strong mages of various forms. Following their studies, Flumphs tend to lead a nomadic lifestyle, and can be found all across Mazrea, though they avoid the colder lands.

The Genasi Homeland is in Beldania, in the land of Urlana. Since their coming to be, however, Genasi have spread across the whole world, and are second only to humans in their diversity and their variety of homeplaces. The Homeland was created during the collision of two Primordial beings, one of Water and one of Earth. To this day, the Genasi worship the Primordials as Gods, and as such have a very naturalistic essence at the core of their being. A water Genasi, for example, may for most of their lives operate as one of the aristocracy in a city, but will still retain a part of themselves that is truly elemental.

The Genasi have a tendency to marry within their chosen element, though it is possible for a Fire and Earth Genasi, for example, to breed. Should this occur, it is up to the twists of fate which element the child shall be born closest to. The Genasi Homeland is a place of primal nature, with cascading waterfalls, volcanic explosions, constant earthquakes and terrifying winds, nevertheless, all Genasi, regardless of where they reside, regard this as home.

Air Genasi

Air Genasi on DNDBeyond

Air Genasi tend to be flighty and temperamental, often chasing their dreams and leaning towards the more creative arts. Many Air Genasi travel, which in turn is a little unusual for Genasi as a whole, as they often find a home that feels elementally in tune with them and settle down. Air Genasi can often be quick to laugh and quick to anger, and rarely hold a grudge or stick to one thing for any long period of time. They are widespread throughout Mazrea, though it is worth mentioning that beyond the Homeland there is also a large collection of Air Genasi in the Storm King’s Citadel in the Archipelago, where the Storm King is an Air Genasi.

Earth Genasi

Earth Genasi on DNDBeyond

Earth Genasi tend to be stoic, determined and slow to rouse, though when they do express their emotions it tends to be a strong and prolonged release. Often gruff and deep-voiced, Earth Genasi are the least travelled of the Genasi, and the most populous within the Homeland. They do not tend to be ambitious, and rarely push for roles of authority or leadership, though they are steady custodians when handed the reins of power. Many Earth Genasi find roles or jobs in life that are close to the earth and ground they feel at home in, whether working in mines and caves, or with the shaping of the stone, or simply surrounded by nature. Earth Genasi tend to be rocky and craggy in features, their skin rough and stonelike, their hair often grown in dreadlocks or thick manes.

Fire Genasi

Fire Genasi on DNDBeyond

Fire Genasi tend to be exactly what you would expect, typically fiery, passionate and emotionally driven. By far the most well-travelled of the genasi, and found all over the world, fire genasi tend to follow their hearts with little consideration for much else, and are often considered rash and reckless. Fire Genasi are a magical folk, and many of them have innate magical powers, which they may or may not further during study or practice. Fire Genasi are often ambitious and power-hungry, though once they attain power they tend not to hold onto it for very long, or seek further power.

Water Genasi

Water Genasi on DNDBeyond

Usually peaceful and meditative in nature, Water Genasi are a gentle folk for the most part, with a calm rationale and a reflective temperament. Water Genasi are found throughout Mazrea, and many that choose to travel do so for religious purposes, feeling the call to share their religion and calling elsewhere. Water Genasi are often powerful Clerics or Druids, their innate magic lending well to their studies. Water Genasi often hold high positions in temples or clergies, but rarely within politics, usually choosing to stay out of such matters. Most Water Genasi make their homes close to the water or on it, and it's very common to find sailors and captains at sea that are Water Genasi.

Gnomes on DNDBeyond

Gnomes are spread throughout the world, and can vary greatly in their nature; nevertheless there is one trait that can link them all together, and that is curiosity. Gnomes are often found as students of the various arts, be they magical or scientific. They are a people who love to create, and to build, and the cities within the countries that gnomes rule tend to be incredibly complicated and magnificent to behold (though not always particularly organised!). The Forest Gnomes tend towards a more peaceful life, and are usually found dwelling on the edges of more magical forests. The Rock Gnomes tend towards the crystalline groves at the foothills of mountains, and tend to be more of the magical arts. The Deep Gnomes tend towards the Underdark and live further from the rest of society than other gnomes. Less is known about these gnomes, for they venture rarely to the surface, but it is said they are the most incredible of builders, and have a symbiotic relationship with the Earth.

Goliaths on DNDBeyond

Goliaths are native to the Gaerik Northlands, and they thrive in this harsh bitter land of thin air and frigid winds. They wander this bleak realm of wind, cold and mountain, and their bodies resemble the harsh lands in which they live. They tend to have very deep voices that rumble like the mountains themselves, and their skin is hardened to be almost rock-like. They prefer the colder climates and those that have left the Northlands have found it difficult to adjust to the temperature changes. The Goliath Tribes attribute themselves to a certain animal, much like their Firbolg cousins and rivals.

In the Cosmos, Goliath and Firbolg Gods are constantly at war, however both follow many of the same beliefs, are both protectors of the land on which they live, and live a life that remains very close to the land and earth. The Goliath Tribes tend to move with the Caribou which they hunt and eat, and each tribe resembles their totem animal in one way or another. Goliaths are always competing, be it with each other, themselves, or the wilderness in which they live. Their competitions are sacred to them, and winners of these competitions are remembered for years after their successes. The Goliaths have a strict code of honor and trust, and lying is a completely foreign concept to them.

Grung on DNDBeyond

There are two types of Grung, the Smallfolk who originate in Chimvaya, and the Tallfolk which are tribal hunters on the islands of the Archipelago. Grung are superstitious folk who believe in portents and omens, and much of their lifestyle reflects this. Rare is the Grung found outside his tribe, although those that travel tend to do so for religious reasons. Most Grung, even the Smallfolk from Chimvaya, follow the Aspects of the Moons.

Hadozee on DNDBeyond

Hadozee are primarily found in the southern islands of the Archipelago; in fact almost the entire population of Hadozee in Mazrea can be found in the Cartara Wilds. Hadozee are a curious folk, who are timid and peaceful in nature, and are very welcoming of travellers. Hadozee tend to be distrustful of magic, unless it is the natural magics of the forests of Endreach. They mostly live in trees, or small houses built within trees. Hadozee are not a very fecund folk, and many families do not have children, making a Hadozee child a precious and well protected creature. This is the main reason Hadozee are rare elsewhere in the world, there are few of them, and they are reluctant to leave the protection of their forests.

Half-Elves on DNDBeyond

A blend of the long-lived Elves and the shorter, hastier humankind, Half-Elves bring traits of both. Belonging fully to neither race, Half-Elves often resemble most the parent that brought them up, though often feel like outcasts and misfits with peers of either race. Many Half-Elves wander the world lonely, or settle into society as quieter folk. Each Half-Elf is different depending on which group of Elves and Humans they were born to.

Half-Orcs on DNDBeyond

The Half-Orcs are, as far we know, unique to Yaervul, a small area of West Gallania. A long distant ancestry of when Orcs and Humans mingled, they share racial traits of both races, though they tend not to interbreed with any true Humans or Orcs anymore. A deeply traditional folk, whose larger and more grotesque faces hide a gentle people who are contemplative, reflective and generous of spirit. The Half-Orcs have shunned the Gods of the Orcs and Humans alike, instead preferring to worship the spirits of the land around them.

Deeply entwined with the nature of the land of Yaervul, most Half-Orcs have a sense of the world around them in the Desert lands that they would be hard pressed to explain to an outsider; somewhere between heat sense and tremorsense. They are astute within the deserts but would be mostly lost outside of them. Yaervul has become as distrustful of outsiders as people have been historically of the Half-Orcs. They follow a strict Hierarchy of age, and the Elders of the community are deeply respected.

Halflings on DNDBeyond

The Halflings are a widespread folk, who can be divided into their nine clans; The Boulderdowns, Burrows, Goldenshires, Safeton and Weylynd Clans are all based in certain places in the world, and tend to remain in these places for generations on end. These Halflings tend to be gentle folk, who live simple and peaceful lives; often artisans or masters of their craft, even if that craft be as simple as farming. There is then the Myrwater Clan, the Royal Clan of Cormyn, who also have members in Weylynd in Kelaan where they rule there too. The Myrwaters are a kind and generous clan, though are a lot less humble and more self-confident than most other Halflings.

The most adventurous of Halflings are the Swiftfoot Clan, who venture all over the world, usually as bards, tricksters and thieves, and will go from place to place telling tales of their travels and investigating new lands. The Swiftfoot Clan members rarely stay in one place for more than a few years before moving on to see the wider world. Much akin to the Swiftfoot Clan are those of the Grinning Ponies; the Grinning Ponies are a group who spend their whole lives raising and breeding Ponies, and traveling upon said ponies to find new places and explore. Much like their Swiftfoot Brothers, Grinning Ponies Halflings rarely stay in one place for a long period of time.

Raezon Halflings are the final clan of halflings, and by far the most unusual. This group of Halflings tend towards the more mystic or Divine arts, and are often found to be sorcerers, wizards and priests. They spend the majority of the lives in study or in devotion, and will often leave their homeland to travel miles across the world to a place that they feel is more natural to their studies and desires.

Harengon on DNDBeyond

Harengon are a rabbit-like humanoid race once based only in Chimvaya, though having since ventured to much of the Archipelago. They tend to be fun-loving tricksters and mischievous, but never violent. Harengon are often fiercely intelligent and their quick thinking is practiced continually in the various games and challenges they play with each other. Harengons are notoriously lucky, full of energy and can vary between 1 and 4 foot in height.

Humans on DNDBeyond

Humans are all over the world, and are as varied as any race can be. Humans have been capable of the most heinous of evil crimes and the greatest of noble deeds; they are one of the few races that tolerate poverty, and allow members of their own race to starve and go homeless within their vast cities. Humans have managed to spread through most of the more temperate countries of Mazrea, though they have struggled to gain any foothold on the lands of Gallania.

Most Human cities are multicultural and diverse, as most humans are very open-minded and accepting of others, despite the way they isolate members of their own communities. Their cities are led by nobility and bloodline is often an important factor in the fate of a human. It can be impossible to judge the nature of any human by their race alone; they follow many Gods, many traditions and varied beliefs depending on where in the world they are born, their class, stature and wealth.

Kalashtar on DNDBeyond

The Kalashtari originated in South East Kelaan, but nowadays they travel freely across all of the world, for they are naturally explorers and wanderers at heart. Part of this is because of their nature, but for the most part this is because at the age of 20, when a Kalashtar comes of age, they go to seek out a place of natural affinity to renew their bond with the spirit that they are bound to from birth. Kalashtari are bound to prime elemental spirits when they are first born; these can be as simple as Earth, Fire and Air spirits, or as complex as Decay, Grazeland, Desert and Waterfall spirits. Each of these spirits has its’ own nature, and though these spirits do not talk, they can communicate feelings. This can lead to Kalashtari taking on certain personality traits from their spirits. A Kalshatar bound to an Earth Spirit for example may be a patient and gentle person, slow to anger; quite the opposite may be true of one bound to a Fire Spirit.

Kalashtar tend to stop wandering when they are due to have a child, and settle down for twenty years or so, before continuing on their travels when their offspring makes their spirit journey to renew their affinity with their spirit. Just because parents have a certain nature of spiritual bond (perhaps they are both bound to water spirits) it does not mean that their children will also have a similar nature. Most Kalashtar find themselves adventuring across the land sooner or later as the wandering urges from their natural spirit companions press on them to do so. A few, however, settle in a place natural to the spirit they are bound with, and remain there for the rest of their years.

Kalashtar are usually a little taller than your average humanoid, usually between 5”8 and 7 feet tall. They tend to be thin and willowy in nature. They tend to live approximately 120-140 years, and when they die, someone must take the spirit that they were bound to to a place that is natural to them to lay it to rest so it may return to the wilds and bind once again to a Kalashtar in the future.

Kender on DNDBeyond

The Kender are magical folk with supernatural curiosity and a fearless tendency to explore. They often enjoy collecting strange objects from their travels and are often explorers, adventurers and archaeologists. Due to their natural inquisitiveness, Kender are often found near ruins, dungeons, and places of magical mystery. Kender are found throughout the world of Mazrea, and do not keep their intimate relationships to members of their own race, meaning that there are many half-Kender of different varieties across the world.

Kenku on DNDBeyond

The Kenku are unusual in Mazrea in that they tend to all be members of the same tribe. Living in the cold, harsh Gaerik Northlands on the South West of the Island, the Kenku are deeply entwined with their worship of the Raven Queen. Much like the Raven Queen, they are a little unusual, and some might say unhinged. Most Kenku worship madness and chaos, and enjoy strange games of intrigue and corruption of the mind. They partake in strong narcotics and alcohols and regularly fraternize with members of the Spirit Realms.

Kenku are unable to fly, a curse from their Raven Queen, but they are an incredibly tough and robust race that survive in a land most would struggle within. There is little trade between them and the other folk of the Gaerik Northlands, but some Kenku have been called by the Raven Queen to spread word of her and have left the Gaerik Northlands on a Pilgrimage to other parts of the world.

Kobolds on DNDBeyond

The Kobolds have been entirely enslaved by the Gnomes. The majority of Kobolds live in Vorzania under the tight control of the Gnomes, though there are a few who have escaped to freedom within the boundaries of Peweston and the Glurvyndale where slavery is not tolerated. The Kobolds believe in the mighty Dragon Gods, and hope that one day their Gods will come down to rain the elements upon the gnomes and free the Kobolds forevermore. There are also Kobolds enslaved underground by the Deep Gnomes and by the Drow. It is incredibly rare to see a free Kobold, and in the lands of the Gnomes they are treated as a necessity. Obedient and educated slaves are well valued within the Gnome lands, so they do their best to ensure their slaves are happy and well taught, but slavery is never pleasant.

Leonin on DNDBeyond

The Leonin are a proud and diverse race, primarily based in Haellian in Beldania, though some few have traveled around Mazrea to other areas. They are a deeply spiritual folk who follow the stars and worship the constellations. Leonin divide themselves into various Prides, who are usually made up of 12-16 Leonin, though some groups are smaller or larger. The Leonin believe in strength of arm, skill at the hunt and have an intensely complicated code of honor.

Most Leonin are nomadic, though there are areas in which certain Prides reside and have done so for many years. Leonin are territorial, and it is customary to send word ahead of a visit. They distrust magic, and all things magical, and pay homage to various monuments within Haellian. Water is a sacred resource to the Prides of the Leonin, and to fight near water is to incur the wrath of all Leonin and their Gods, or so it is believed. Water, and Desert Oases, are sacred and peaceful places where the Prides can gather to share tales and find partners.

Lizardfolk on DNDBeyond

The Lizardfolk are a race who seem to rush to complete as much as they can in their shorter lifespans than any other race. They are deeply ambitious, and apply this to whatever their trade is at the time. Most Lizardfolk see politics as a means to power and accomplishment, though Lizardfolk worldwide have been seen applying this ambition to all manners of trades and lifestyles. They are a hardy folk, and thrive in varied environments, from jungle to deserts. Jungle Lizardfolk are renowned for the narcotics that they both grow and trade, the Yuan-ti are especially fond of these narcotics and in areas of jungle you can almost always find lizardfolk and Yuan-ti living side by side.

The Lizardfolk are often quick to violence, much as they are quick to any other action within their lives. Most lizardfolk seem to try to sleep as little as possible, do as much as they can within their days, and repeat this the following day.

Loxodon on DNDBeyond

The Loxodon are a peaceful tribal folk, though each of their tribes are in very different places throughout Mazrea. Loxodon are incredibly loyal to each other and to their ancestry, and each tribe keeps incredibly detailed histories of their ancestry. They can be found in the Gaerik Northlands, the extreme West in Gallania and in South-East Kelaan. Rare is the Loxodon who leaves their tribe, though it has been known to have happened. Loxodon tribes are Matriarchal, and they tend to defer to their female compatriots at all times. Loxodon stand between 7 and 8 foot tall for the most part, are exceptionally heavy and live for 4 or 5 centuries.

Minotaurs on DNDBeyond

Minotaurs tend to be guards, soldiers and mercenaries, being naturally inclined towards the warriors’ arts. Minotaurs are very diverse in nature, some bearing large horns like a bull and some none at all. Most minotaurs revel in jewellery and treasure and have a primal desire to collect beauty. Minotaurs are mostly found in the richer cities of the Archipelagos, on boats upon the Sea of Rogues or in the grand cities of Kelaan.

Mousefolk on DNDBeyond

A peaceful folk from the land of Chimvaya, Mousefolk spend much of their days eating and sleeping. They tend to have extremely large families and value bloodlines and family over all else. Usually found fairly close to the World Tree, they see themselves as the protectors of the tree, and spend the vast majority of their time caring for the tree and polishing it up and down its mighty limbs. The Mousefolk stand between 1 and 3 foot tall, and are divided into the Meadow folk, who live at the bottom of the tree, and the Softpaws, who make their homes within the World Tree itself. Mousefolk tend to be resourceful and tenacious, and they believe in community over all else. The patterns of their fur dictate their hereditary history and family bloodlines and thus they keep incredibly good care of their fur. They are traders, craftsmen and hunters and are as close to a warrior as you might find within Chimvaya.

Some mousefolk have ventured forth from Chimvaya to Northern Kelaan where they have become renowned as chefs and food producers. There’s also a fair contingent of mousefolk throughout the Archipelago who have found the call of adventure irresistible.

Naga on DNDBeyond

Naga are snakelike creatures in their demeanour as much as their features. Cunning, sly and ambitious, they are often found in small tribes in the Savage West of Gallania, or as lone enterprising individuals scattered throughout the Archipelago and Kelaan. The naga found in cities and towns are often regarded suspiciously and may well offer services and products that would not be readily available elsewhere. Naga tend to be narcissistic and untrustworthy, though there are exceptions. It is very difficult to tell a female and male Naga apart.

Owlins on DNDBeyond

The Owlin are a strange and mystic folk who inhabit the trees of Chimvaya. They keep mostly to themselves, and believe in the wisdom of the collective above all else. Most Owlin spend their lives in study and in contemplation, whether that study is of history, the arcane arts or the mystic crafts of their people. The Owlin worship the Goddess of the Night above all else, and almost all Owlin are nocturnal.

The plumage of an Owlin develops over time with their age and magics, and a multi-coloured Owlin is assumably both aged and powerful in the ways of their magics. Owlin are often incredibly protective of their secrets and their homes and rarely venture to speak with the other races of Chimvaya. Those Owlin who have left Chimvaya have often travelled to the Archipelago and beyond, heeding their natural curiosity and desire for knowledge. There is also a small contingent of Owlin wizards in the Academy of Shar.

Satyrs on DNDBeyond

The Satyr tend to be a nomadic race, operating in small clans across Mazrea. Satyr can be divided into four groups by the God they worship. Those that worship Gaecilia are the gentle forest Satyr, mostly found in North-West Kelaan. Those that worship Jurus are those that tend towards the more mischievous and criminal lives, though there are also many clerics of Jurus who are not criminal at all. There are those that worship Borlean and study the arcane arts, or become tradesfolk of the more complex type, such as apothecaries and alchemists. Most City Satyr worship Borlean. Lastly, there are those that worship Quan. The Quan-worshipping Satyr are the nomadic, fun-loving, all singing, all dancing Satyrs for which the race is most well known. These Satyr can be found wandering between towns and cities, performing on the road and in taverns and generally worshiping life, love and laughter.

Shifters on DNDBeyond

Shifters are found in the Savage West of Gallania and in Southern Kelaan (they were once the defenders of the borders of Zurei when it was independent). They are a fierce folk, excellent in battle and well known for their fearsome beastly transformations. Despite this, however, outside of battle the shifters are mostly a peaceful folk who worship the land and their totem animals. Shifters are often on the edge of wildness and can be a little chaotic however. The Shifters believe in being self-reliant and conjuring inner strength in times of need rather than relying on someone else. A common saying for a shifter is “preparing for the journey yet to come” which reflects how they believe the world changes constantly and it's important to be prepared for whatever comes.

Shifters believe that the reward for this independence is freedom, and will always feel uncomfortable around strict authority for these reasons; Shifters often don’t meld well into settlements with complex law structures. Shifters often change moods in swift and dramatic fashion and find it difficult to control their emotions in stressful situations. Shifters often take on personality traits of their totem animals without meaning to; it is not uncommon to see a shifter snarl or growl.

Simic Hybrids on DNDBeyond

The Simic Hybrids are limited to only the land of Endreach in the Great Archipelago. Many centuries ago the West end of the island rose up due to a volcano from deep under the sea, tilting the land at an angle into the ocean. All those living in the East side of the island were lowered far below the ocean due to this catastrophe, but their God took pity on them, and the great Aquoris sheltered them with a hand. Wherever his hand touched these beings, their previously Sea Elf bodies changed forevermore in various different ways creating the Simic Hybrid race. They are distrustful of outsiders, very warlike and martial in their outlook and stick to old traditions that many others would deem savage.

Tabaxi on DNDBeyond

The Tabaxi are split into two separate groups. There are the standard Desert Tabaxi that you can find in various places across the world of Mazrea. These Tabaxi tend to be a peaceful race that inhabit the mountainous and desert lands of Gallania, Beldania and Kelaan. They have strict traditions and respect their Elders; following their guidance no matter what. Tabaxi often protect almost untouched ruins that were once revered by their people within their lands. They stand between 5 and 6 foot tall and are incredibly agile. Deep in the South in Llornikasia are the Snow Tabaxi. A much untouched wildland within the snow, the Tabaxi there have adapted incredibly well. They see few outsiders and speak a unique and unusual language. Remarkably, however, their traditions and beliefs echo those of the Desert Tabaxi in many ways.

Thri-Kreen on DNDBeyond

Thri-kreen resemble bipedal praying mantises. Their bodies are coated in exoskeletons with various earthy hues, including rust red, sandy yellow, and mottled green-brown. Unlike most humanoids, thri-kreen possess six limbs, walking on two legs and possessing four arms, each ending in four prehensile, claw-like fingers. The lower two arms are smaller and less-powerful than the upper two. Their heads possess compound eyes, generally in somewhat-brighter colours like green or blue, antennae, and mandibles. Because of the alien shape of their jaws, they have difficulty speaking and pronouncing Common and most other humanoid languages besides their own.

Almost exclusively found in the Swelled Tangles of the Archipelago, Thri-Kreen are a secretive people who are unwelcoming to outsiders. With fierce and capable warriors and a small amount of magic, most Thri-Kreen tribes are formidable enemies. What is known about them is that they have a strict tribe hierarchy, and that this applies across all the different tribes of the Tangles. Thri-Kreen have a very rigid code of honour that they adhere to even when others might deem it madness.

Tieflings on DNDBeyond

Once apparently descended from Devils and Humans conceiving children together, nowadays the Tieflings live peacefully within cities alongside other races. Tieflings are known to be quick to laughter and joy and are often playful and mischievous. Most tieflings have red-tinged skin with deep black eyes and horns. There are, however, an incredibly wide variety of complexions, horn types and eye colours within the Tiefling culture. They are adaptable and resilient to hardship, and possess strong aptitude at whatever pursuit they dedicate themselves to, whether that be fighting, art, or anything else.

When facing adversity, the tiefling instinct is not to withdraw from the world, but rather to challenge it head-on. Tieflings seek to make their own fates, defy the odds, and take risks that even most humans would not dare. They are fiercely independent and as such Tieflings are known to make intractable slaves.

Tieflings can be found throughout Mazrea, though are often regarded negatively or shunned in Kelaan. Tieflings tend to worship the Constellation, though many are drawn specifically to Canis, the God of Ambition. Tieflings are often naturally magical and many of them pursue the arcane arts as a result.

Tortles on DNDBeyond

The Tortles are a very solitary race, maturing within a few years of their birth and setting out traveling around the world. Tortles are not a very populous race, but its not unusual to find one wandering through the cities and wilderness anywhere in the world of Mazrea. They are a long-lived race, often living up to a millenia in age, and they tend to be slow to conflict, instead preferring to ponder both sides of any given argument. There are almost no Tortles in positions of responsibility, most instead preferring to remain as observers within the world. Tortles tend towards the more magical and divine arts rather than defending themselves with martial abilities.

Triton on DNDBeyond

In the Great Archipelago, there is no race more commonly seen than the Triton. Existing both on the small islands of the Archipelago and in their underwater cities, the island chains are the perfect home for this race. The Tritons are a fierce race of individuals well adapted to aquatic environments. They are a matriarchal society who have deep religious beliefs that they follow faithfully and ardently. Their underwater cities are incredibly beautiful to any who are able to visit, though they rarely ever let outsiders visit. A proud people with many preconceptions as a result of their way of life who believe the world owes them due to the evils under the sea that this race single-handedly keep at bay. Perhaps they are right.

Vedalken on DNDBeyond

The Vedalken inhabit the cold lands of the Gaerik Northlands, where they live in their fortresses beneath the icy tundra, working endlessly and tirelessly to create unusual contraptions and make breakthroughs in magical discovery. A race who are never content with where they are, but instead seek endless improvement and a perfection that cannot be achieved, the Vedalken are fiercely intelligent. They mature slowly and age very slowly, often living many centuries; they resemble the environments they live in in many different ways; their skin the pale blue of the icy caverns they live under, moving and aging slowly like the frozen tundras above. Ultimately, the Vedalken are a group that rarely travel or entertain visitors, though they do maintain trade relations with the Loxodon and Goliath tribes near their homes. They place intellectual prowess and understanding above all else, and this is reflected by the Gods they worship.

Yuanti on DNDBeyond

The Yuanti are cold blooded in more ways than one. Found almost exclusively within the jungles of Beldania, the Archipelago and Kelaan, the Yuanti are a threat to nobody but themselves because of their lifestyles. Spending almost the entirety of their existences plotting against one another for political and financial gain and planning each others’ assassinations, this uncaring and calculated race are often so enmeshed in their political agendas that they forget anything else matters. Often addicted to various narcotics grown within the jungles surrounding them, as many of them use anti-toxins to avoid being poisoned as pleasure drugs, it is rare to find a Yuanti with a clear mind. Those that do manage to achieve a clear mind for a brief period of time often leave the jungles to pursue different lives outside of Yuanti politics. Yuanti are fiercely loyal to their individual clans and bloodlines.

Whilst enough Harengon, Mousefolk and Owlin have made their way throughout the world of Mazrea that they are not considered unusual or rare anymore, some of the other races originating in Chimvaya have not spread far enough across the land to be known or recognised around the world. These include Badgerfolk, Duckfolk, Otterfolk, Squirrelfolk, Cervan, Corvum, Gallus, Hedge, Luma, Mapach, Raptors and Vulpin. These races can be found in Mazrea outside of Chimvaya, but are incredibly rare and unusual. Gallus, for example, are limited to a very small community of seven or eight families within the Archipelago and are unknown anywhere else outside of Chimvaya.

The Lands of Mazrea

Gallania is the Land of Dragons; throughout history Gallania has been a land shared between Dragons and humanoids. The majority of the population of Gallania has always been Dragonborn, and there has never been a definitive ruler or leader of the nation as a whole, instead being broken down into the territories of various warlords, clans and dynasties.

Much of Gallania has been settled and administered by these various clans and dynasties without change for decades or even centuries; not all territories are fought over anymore. Most of these dynasties are areas surrounding the primary city or town of that clan, for example Ushma has an area around it owned by the Ushma clan, that then borders with Jeras and Lahas and their surrounding territories.

There are, however, areas of Gallania where border skirmishes and clan fighting has been happening constantly for much of history. Some of these are formal battles that take place on specific dates with rules and regulations to prevent too much unnecessary loss of lives. Others are subject to continued brutal and violent skirmishes and raids throughout the year that cause relentless pressure on both clans.

Over the last 100 years, a Warlord named Kyza’an, a black Dragonborn with a strong prowess for magic who rose to the leadership of Clan Vazra, began to not only skirmish with clans at his border, but invade them fully. Much of the land that he invaded was once known as the Savage Lands, lands belonging to non-Dragonborn clans and tribes who, in the past, protected the land stubbornly and forcefully. During these last 100 years, much of Northern Gallania has been changed by the war, and the lands are now known as the Savage West and the territory of Vazra, with other cities and towns having been built by Kyza’an, who called himself Emperor.

Despite the violent nature of the civil war causing many issues across Northern Gallania, Emperor Kyza’an also provided huge opportunities for many of the common folk of the land, whether through employment in his armies, or in the building of his grand cities and their buildings, or through huge improvements to education, infrastructure and administration of these lands.

Beldania is a naturally magical land divided into countries with fairly strict borders. Until recently, these countries were divided by the races that inhabited them, though over the last half-century or so diversity within the countries has increased. Even so, many within Mazrea see a specific country of Beldania as their home land (the Genasi even call the city in Beldania “Homeland”).

Beldania has a very diverse collection of Biomes, from the Haellian desert to the giant forests of Vuralia, the magically dense crystal jungle of Gzellen and the drylands of Muegos. Yukken is a dense jungle, and there are various mountain ranges throughout Beldania. Rivers flowing from various places in Beldania often converge at the Lake of Stars, at the centre of which sits a giant whirlpool.

Beldania is regarded as “the first land” by many, and in the time before The Constellation, when Old Gods walked the lands, Beldania was thought to be the only inhabited land. Certainly the history of Elves (which tends to predate most other histories) begins in Beldania. For such a small land, more tragedy, catastrophe and change has come from Beldania than anywhere else in the world.

Beldania is also home to the Manufactory, a large company formed by 3 artificers long ago that is now the biggest producer of magical items in the world, and the only company to mass-produce and distribute potions and magical goods. The Manufactory also retains strong ties to the Academy of Shar, a school of magic renowned worldwide where all formidable (and wealthy) magic users study for at least some period of time. The Academy sits on the island of Shar, a short distance from the shores of Beldania.

Also a small island off the coast of Beldania, Mymna-Kolrik has become more known to the general world in recent times. A land full of seers and mystics, many now visit to find their fortune, locate someone or something they have lost or seek to twist fate to their own ends. Despite its reputation as a land of danger and mystery (or perhaps because of it), and the fact many who seek Mymna-Kolrik’s shores do not make it, it has become an attractive destination for the rich, the ambitious and the foolish.

Kelaan is a land of riches and storied history. Although much of the beauty and magnificence of the grand cities, spectacular architecture and infrastructure of Kelaan is tainted by slavery, it is unquestionable that Kelaan is a land of beauty and power. Power has always been a struggle throughout Kelaan, and the land was once divided into various countries, which now make up the regions of Kelaan.

The capital of Kelaan is Galvynia, and there is no larger city in all of Mazrea, and the palace of Galvynia is a masterclass in luxury, opulence and lavish display of wealth and power. Much of Kelaan is divided into the estates and surrounding lands of the wealthy and powerful, and the towns and cities they have built.

There is a fair amount of natural beauty in Kelaan, specifically in the Glurvyndale, the lakes and mountains of Morendral, and the beautiful coastline of Western Kelaan. These natural lands are preserved in their beauty and laws protect them from urban development.

There is a vast cultural and racial diversity within Kelaan, with almost all races of Mazrea found somewhere in the large cities and towns. The population of Kelaan is many times larger than that of any other lands of Mazrea, and the city of Galvynia houses more people than many small countries elsewhere.

Whilst the rulership of Kelaan unquestionably belongs to the Sha’Rizai family, the politics of the dukes, lords and other titled nobles of the land are vicious and dangerous. Kelaan is a land where the class system is openly visible to everyone, the wealth disparity is shocking and the disregard for human life is often horrifying, all cloaked in an opulence of affluence and luxury.

The Archipelago of Mazrea is a strange amalgamation of dozens of different cultures, biomes, lands and populations with vast contrast throughout. From the dense forests of the Cartara Wilds in Endreach to the opulent city of Goldtown, from the Shifting Sands of Phorek and the sprawling bazaars of Maregal to the sparse icelands of Nenra and Narthuul, the Archipelago is as varied as possible.

There is no one designated ruler of any particular area of the Archipelago, though there are many lands with different politics and government depending on their size, location and administration. The Storm Mist Citadel is home to the Storm King who has absolute rule there, Goldtown is ruled by the Conclave of Lords, Endreach is mostly a tribal land with no rulers but Maregal is unquestionably ruled by the Sultan, Durbryn is a proud free port though the Four Ships are certainly responsible for its administration.

In Aerwell, the chieftain can only be a woman and she fights for her right to rule through a series of battles and strength challenges. Astrium has had a king for generations, though he doesn’t assert his right to rule and leaves most of the administration of Voldarr to his parliament. Yveslen has been ruled by the same elf for a millenia and the Glimmer is the safest forest in the entirety of Mazrea, whereas in the Swelled Tangles a Thri-Kreen tribe may well kill you on sight.

As such, the Archipelago is a place of adventure, opportunity and diversity with a healthy dose of danger. It is vital to understand the microcosm of government, power or politics that you are visiting, whilst also understanding the natural dangers. There are more deadly predators, wild elementals and dangerous men in the Archipelago than anywhere else in the world, and the natural dangers are no less. From stormy seas and ice floes to relentless desert sands, dense jungles of dangerous plants to perilous frozen crags, black wastelands of volcanic ash to deadly marshes and bogs, the natural dangers of the Archipelago are constant and trying.

Within this strange conglomeration of unusual governments, anarchies and pirates, natural and unnatural dangers and strange customs are countless places of beauty, cultural diversity and adventure. For the aspiring hero, mercenary group of adventuring troupe, no land offers more chances for fame and riches, with countless unexplored ruins, ancient mage’s towers and dark dungeons, with the remnants of hundreds of civilizations and leaders that faded into obscurity, the Archipelago is as enticing as it is deadly.

Chimvaya is a myth to many in the lands of Mazrea, though accurate histories and old maps will tell you what it was and where it once was. The critters of Chimvaya will tell you that its still there, if you can call it home. Chimvaya is a land of perfect peace and harmony, a community of various critter-like creatures and the world tree living in unity. Whilst there are scatterings of the various critters of Chimvaya throughout Mazrea, and a few do leave the island each generation, for the most part, Chimvaya is a unique land of complete isolation from the rest of the world.

Chimvaya is aware of the outer world, though for many millennia it was not, but it is uninterested mostly, instead preferring the peace and joy of the land of plenty. Chimvaya has very little influence on the rest of the world, and most of the rest of the world are unaware of its existence.

The Gaerik Northlands is the large snow-covered land to the far North of Mazrea and the few islands that cling to it. These lands are treacherous and cold, with vast stretches of barren lands between the small towns and fishing villages that cling to their existences across the continent. The Vedalken, Goliaths, Kenku and some tribes of Loxodon make these lands their home, whilst others either visit or attach themselves to one of these communities.

Only Xandrik has become a multicultural and diverse place over time, mostly based on the fishing trade there, though over time other rare goods have been traded there that are thought of as commonplace in the Northlands, such as scrimshaw and a rare silver stone that emanates heat.

Llarnikasia is a barren land of giant mountains and ice cliffs with shifting floes and regular earthquakes. In addition, there is one of the largest volcanoes in Mazrea, known simply as Infernus, that erupts two or three times a decade. There is one single walled city on Llarnikasia, called Urveswallyn, though it is often completely abandoned. It is believed to belong to the nomadic snow Tabaxi who are indigenous to the lands, and who follow the caribou on their seasonal migrations. Very few visit these lands, and those that do often report strange happenings and unusual phenomena that do not necessarily match up with other reports.

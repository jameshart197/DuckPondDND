Culture in Mazrea

For the most part, culture is very separated by country, city or even village. It is worth mentioning however, that some races are regarded as ‘evil’ throughout the world, such as goblinfolk, orcs, and drow. Creatures of these races will find it difficult to enter cities, and must work very hard to earn the trust of their respective civilizations.

Religion is a key part of the culture of much of the world, and it is incredibly unusual to find somebody that does not follow any religion. Festivals, customs, traditions and even laws are often based on the practice and doctrine of the religions observed in those areas. Clerics and Priests are regarded with respect throughout Mazrea, even when serving a religion not observed in the area. There is very little rivalry between the varying Pantheons of Mazrea, and indeed in some places, often in the Archipelago, multiple religions are observed in the same place.

In terms of the relationships between the different races of Mazrea, for the most part diversity is prevalent throughout the world, and most cities contain a wide variety of races which live peacefully within their walls. That being said, there are some races who are renowned for their casual racism and xenophobia; the Dwarves often regard any non-Dwarf as suspicious or simply uninteresting, Elves often regard many other races as simply youthful or inferior, and the Vedalken take racial arrogance to the extreme, regarding all races other than Vedalken as simple and poorly-formed. There are also some races who are treated unanimously badly throughout the world; Kobolds are enslaved throughout most of Mazrea, Bugbears and Minotaurs are often regarded as lacking sufficient intellect, and Yuan-ti are regarded with suspicion and fear (though most would say this reputation is fairly earned).

Throughout most of Mazrea, while there are folk who are regarded as ‘poor’, these people would not lack the basic necessities to live. In Kelaan and Maregal (in the Archipelago) however, abject poverty is commonplace and many die of starvation, improper hygiene, or lack of basic necessities. What is thought of as unconscionable in Beldania or Gallania, to ignore those who are suffering, is an art perfected within Kelaan especially. Likewise, the hoarding of wealth beyond what can be reasonably spent or used seems to be limited to similar places, though those of evil weal are an exception to this rule (such as the Leygon twins).

Whilst slavery is not illegal anywhere in the world except for Chimvaya, it is regarded with distaste in Beldania, some of the Archipelago and the Gaerik Northlands. In Kelaan however, slavery is responsible for much of the opulence and affluence of the country, and the country has progressed far beyond its rivals as a result. Escaped slaves from Kelaan are not often returned to the country by force, unless the owners have paid handsomely for the retrieval of said slaves, and in Beldania the act of forcibly returning slaves to Kelaan is illegal.

Magic in Mazrea

Magic in Mazrea is extremely commonplace, with many of the common folk able to cast a simple cantrip or two. There are many practitioners of the Arcane in Mazrea, though most are small time tricksters, apprentice wizards or those who have learnt a small amount of magic to assist in their daily lives (tradesfolk learning things such as mage hand or mold earth for example).

Those with more magic tend to be wizards who have spent long years researching their arts and gathering spells to their spellbooks. Those to whom magic comes innately, usually referred to as sorcerers, are fairly unusual, and those who receive the Arcane magics in other ways are few and far between. With that being said, there are many non-humanoids who claim the Arcane Arts as part of their nature, though they aren't all that common to see.

There are many churches, temples and other places of worship where one might encounter priests with the ability to cast Divine Magic spells, at least to some extent - Rare is the priest that can cast higher magic such as Greater Restoration, or even raising the dead. Paladins and Clerics of these orders often claim some level of Divine magic also, and there are many Rangers and Druids found throughout the world who have some level of more natural magic.

As such, magic is not regarded with fear or surprise, at least at lower power levels (those that can cast spells of up to second level) however more powerful casters may garner some reputation and become known to authorities in the lands they travel. Throughout Mazrea, laws on magic do differ, however the following are fairly common rulings;

Summoning

It is considered inappropriate to summon creatures of a monstrous nature, or any of whom are demon, devil or undead, within civilised lands. If you do have a servant of an unusual nature, it is appropriate to ask them to wait for you somewhere away from villages and towns. That being said, it is expected that you will use any magic available to you to defend yourself, and should you summon such a creature in defence of your own life, and dismiss it before it can wreak any havoc upon the lands around you, your use of such spells will generally be accepted.

Necromancy

Necromancy, specifically the process of reanimating the dead, or causing a corpse to rise, is considered distasteful in civilised lands, and illegal within bigger towns and cities. It is not uncommon for a temple practitioner to speak with the dead, but to raise them to a state of undeath is almost unanimously feared and abhorred. Those who do have undead servants are advised to disguise them well or risk being feared as a warlock or lich and attacked in defence of the surrounding lands.

Changes to specific spell

The following spells have been removed/banned;

  • Silvery Barbs
  • Comprehend Languages
  • Identify
  • Tongues
  • Distort Value
  • Modify Memory

Detect Magic

When casting this spell, it will only reveal the presence of magic in general. An Arcana check (and perhaps others) will be needed to discern anything further.

Locate Animals/Plants/Creature/Object

These spells are blocked by any lead, as well as by large walls of stone or natural land (such as when the object is deep underground, or within a castle).

All spells with a duration of 8 hours

All spells with a duration of 8 hours will last until the end of your next long rest, regardless of when they were cast.

The Economy of Mazrea

The Economy of Mazrea is fairly stable throughout the world, with four types of currency being most common, and the value of goods remaining fairly balanced throughout Mazrea. Whilst magical goods have become cheaper in Mazrea, especially with the presence of the Manufactory, goods such as these remain out of reach for most of the common folk throughout the land. In some places, gems or other trade goods are accepted in lieu of coinage, though most simpler merchants will ask you to exchange such goods prior to paying.

Prices in taverns and inns are kept fairly consistent, and fairly low, throughout Mazrea as these are the gathering places of all but the lowliest of beggars or the greatest of misers. Work done on demand will always cost substantially more than work done prior to payment, as such weapons can often be found at a fairly manageable price for most who are considering adventure, but good armor is often difficult to come by for all but the wealthiest of adventurers.

The four types of currency in Mazrea are;

  • The Copper Shard - a small piece of copper with a fixing so they can be connected with other shards, ten of which can be put together to form a single large copper piece, is usually reserved for the simplest of goods such as bread roll or single units of widely available goods.
  • The Copper Piece - usually the price of a simple meal in most taverns, these pieces are made up of ten copper shards interconnected. The Copper Piece is a fairly large piece of round copper with a flat face on either side. Ten Copper Pieces form a Silver Piece
  • The Silver Piece - usually the price of a simple weapon, or a night's stay at a tavern, is a fairly small piece wrought of silver, often with its face stamped in the likeness of a leader, god or some form of art. Silver pieces can vary in size, but are always exactly the same weight, and a sensible merchant weighs coinage before accepting payment. Ten Silver Pieces form a Gold Piece
  • The Gold Piece - universal in weight and accepted by everyone, is more than the average family would earn in a month.

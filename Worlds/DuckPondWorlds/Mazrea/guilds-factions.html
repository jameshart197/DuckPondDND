Guilds and Factions in Mazrea

Across the world of Mazrea there are countless guilds and factions dedicated to specific pursuits, such as the various guilds of merchants across the lands, specific orders dedicated to artisan crafts, or the many guilds of adventurers, guards and mercenaries. It should also be mentioned that the University of Vazra and the Academy of Shar, whilst not factions or guilds themselves, have strong influence internationally. That being said, there are a few guilds or factions that operate across the entire world. These are as follows;

Whilst many of the members of the Mages’ Guild have studied at the Academy of Shar, all official members of the Guild no longer have any affiliation with the Academy. The Mages’ Guild operates across Gallania, Beldania, the Archipelago, the Gaerik Northlands and Kelaan. An organisation dedicated to the sharing of magical resources and knowledge, the Guild tries to refrain from engaging in political matters.

There are towers or Guild Halls in most cities across Mazrea, and each of these can provide, for a fee, alchemical equipment, ingredients and components, access to scrolls and tomes, information, and in many cases, teleportation to the other Guild Halls. Members of the Mages’ Guild would also gain access to a selection of Wizard Robes, Arcane Focuses and other simple magical equipment, as well as enchanting services.

There are temples for each Pantheon throughout Mazrea, however some of these are limited to specific areas where the worship of these Gods is most common. In Gallania, the Aspects of the Moons have many places of worship in the Savage West, but there is little acknowledgement of these Gods elsewhere in Mazrea. Similarly, whilst worshipped throughout Mazrea, the Totems have little physical representation within the world; acknowledgement of the religion mostly being through tribal tattoos or adornments.

The Great Dragons have their temples throughout Gallania, but there are few temples to them elsewhere in the world, and the Genasi have placed various temples to the Primordials where they exist in any substantial numbers. The Elves keep to their own ways, often using simpler shrines and sites of natural beauty as places of worship for their own pantheons, though Elven Temples do exist.

The large exception to this is the Temples of the Constellation. Almost any village in Mazrea not dedicated to a specific other religion will have at least a shrine in acknowledgment of the Constellation as a whole. Small towns will have one or more churches or temples that worship the entire Constellation or subsections of the Pantheon. It is not uncommon for certain towns and villages to have a place of worship dedicated to a specific God of the Constellation, and big cities will have a temple dedicated to every one of the twenty-five Gods of the Constellation.

The influence of the Temples of the Constellation, especially the High Priests and Priestesses of the individual Gods, and indeed the High Clergy who preside over the entire temple is not to be underestimated. Their wealth and power alone is formidable in any circle, and their influence over the rulers and politicians of all of the nations of Mazrea is significant. Should the Clergy and the High Priests ever all agree on one single cause or action, it is likely that the world will swiftly follow suit and act accordingly.

The Manufactory is a large company formed by 3 artificers long ago that is now the biggest producer of magical items in the world, and the only company to mass-produce and distribute potions and magical goods. Mostly the Manufactory mass produces weapons capable of damaging ethereal or otherwise difficult to hit creatures, potions with various simple effects, and items of jewellery or clothing that can confer simple magical abilities.

The Manufactory also has an extended range called “The Adventurer’s Selection” that are much higher in cost and not produced on the same scale. These are sold as “all an adventurer needs to survive in the dangerous world of Mazrea” though mostly they are utility items with extra benefits, such as holding pouches with extra-dimensional spaces, easy to access and use tool-belts or tents with resistance to the weather and other dangers.

The Collective are an organization spread across the world with various outposts and guild houses, all dedicated to collecting and storing information and artifacts from the entirety of Mazrea. All of this information is stored in multiple places, but there is a single museum of the Collective in Lorvallen, Beldania that is so big that almost nobody has explored it in full. Although it is assumed that the Collective also have archives they do not make available to the public, anything displayed or accessible to the public is stored in the Collective Museum in Beldania.

There are countless historians, archaeologists, scholars, adventurers, thieves and collectors working for the Collective, seeking artifacts, information, history and details about anything and everything within the world. From detailed information on the races of the world to collections of stones from different mountains, from replicas of the many magical artifacts and weapons of history to poetry libraries, the Collective tries to be “a catalogue of everything there is to know in the world”, though obviously such a library would be impossible to maintain. Nevertheless, the Collective is a valuable resource both to those who wish to study or find information or to those who wish to sell magical goods, information or unusual pieces found throughout the world.

The Most Careful Order of Meteorologists was established after the great Druid Wars of Beldania almost two centuries ago. When the druids in the war created cataclysmic weather effects, they had repercussions felt throughout the world. The Most Careful Order of Meteorologists are a fairly small group of powerful wizards and druids, and a fairly large group of researchers, scryers and observers, who work together to try and maintain some form of equilibrium in the weather and climate of the world. They are often reduced to fairly simple tasks, such as hiring out for ships for the wealthy or assisting farmers and agriculturists, but when powerful weather-changing magic occurs, or natural disasters, they are a faction that retains the respect, admiration and gratitude of many across Mazrea.

The Silver Sword are a group, mostly consisting of Dragonborn, who attempt to maintain peace and safety across the world of Mazrea. Mostly operational in Gallania and Beldania, the group has been known to participate in various world-changing events by lending what aid they can, as well as offering aid and respite to many affected by disaster, war or politics.

The Silver Sword attempts to refrain from being involved in politics, though its stance on freedom, liberty and justice often prevents this. Whilst they do not directly oppose slavery, warlords, political wars or invasion, they are often seen helping those who become the victims of such things.

The Mystic Eye are a guild of seers, mystics and fortune tellers who are based in Mymna-Kolrik, but have guildhouses all over the world. For the most part, they offer divination services for financial compensation, usually in simple tasks such as seeking objects or people who are lost, scrying long distances or even communicating across the continents. In more recent times however, they have become the focus of the wealthy and powerful, who will often pay for auguries or predictions on upcoming decisions, events or battles.

Many of the seers of the Mystic Eye are very truthful about the accuracy of their predictions, and prefer to keep to simple divinations and the loaning of more substantial services, though there are certainly some few charlatans who attempt to profit from the organisation’s recent rise in popularity. This has, in turn, tarnished the reputation of the Mystic Eye and led to the organisation cloaking themselves in secrecy and mystery once again in recent times.

The International College of Bards is an organisation dedicated to the formalisation of spoken history, legal witness and ceremonial assistance by the Bards of the world. Many people, especially in areas of less affluence, will call upon a Bard to witness an agreement or event, or to speak at an important milestone or moment in their lives, and the College attempts to maintain consistency across all of its members.

The College of Bards is also a place where Bards can go to train and learn, especially the traditional bardic verses and songs of old as well as the important historical information a Bard is expected to learn. Its also a place for the study of language and the exchange of information regarding the modernisation and colloquialism throughout the various languages of Mazrea.

The organisation's guild houses are also places of knowledge, often containing vast libraries and huge collections of instruments and music, and are a place for the various Bards of the world to meet with each other and exchange knowledge, art and pleasantries. Members are afforded shelter, assistance with their arts, details of vacancies in courts around the world and in some instances, direct sponsorship for their work. It is also important to note that should a Bard write a new piece of verse or music, they can store a copy at the guild to register their ownership of the piece.

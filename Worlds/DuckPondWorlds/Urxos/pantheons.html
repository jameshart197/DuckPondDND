Pantheons of Urxos

For as long as anyone remembers, the pantheon within Urxos is called The Seven. Seven Gods and Goddesses each with two facets, often representing a darker and lighter side, or some believe, a good and evil side. Each God or Goddess is represented by an image divided in half into the two Facets.

In almost all cases both facets of all of The Seven are worshipped equally, though there are a few cases where superstition can cause a more quiet or respectfully distant form of worship. This is most common with Vileus, who is Father of Life and Death. His Death Aspect is often given a quiet nod, as opposed to fervent worship. Karazan's mischief aspect is often regarded as a God for criminals and thieves, though it doesn't stop children indulging in mischief during his festival times.

Vileus, often referred to as the Father, or the Facet of Life and Death, is depicted as both a kindly man and an almost unknowable, fearful power at the same time. There is much contrast between the Facet of Life, often worshipped by Druids, Clerics and the like for his mastery over healing, nature and growth, and his Facet of Death, often seen as a darker, more evil deity who Necromancers draw their power from.

Vileus is revered throughout the year, but he is especially celebrated during the following festivals; The Day of the Seven, Rainsmeet, Day of Rebirth, The Long Goodbye, Festival of Life and the Harvest Gala. It might be noted that only The Long Goodbye is specifically dedicated to the Facet of Death.

Aever, Bringer of Light and Shadow, is the God of Light and Darkness. One of the more distant and harder to comprehend Gods, Aever is much revered, but one of the least loved of all the Seven.

The Facet of Light is often followed by Clerics of certain orders, Paladins, and many others seeking justice and truth. The Facet of Shadows is often followed by those seeking to hide the truth or hide in shadow, and few are the followers of the Aspect of Shadows who claim a truly Good Alignment.

Aever is revered throughout the year, but he is especially celebrated during the following festivals; The Day of the Seven, Firefest and The Festival of Lights

Amara, Mistress of Love and Lust, is probably the most beloved of the Seven. Both of her Facets are worshipped ardently and regularly, and she is often called upon during all manner of relationships, marriage and sexual interaction. Amara is seen as both a gentle and harsh mistress, but is followed by all but the coldest of hearts.

Amara is worshipped by various Cleric orders devoted to love, lust, fertility, marriage and beauty. She is also seen as the matron of most courtesans and her image is often painted upon bagnios and brothels worldwide.

Amara is revered throughout the year, but she is especially celebrated during the following festivals; The Day of the Seven, Rainsmeet and Rapture, though many would argue she often sees more worship during festival days than the intended Aspect.

Zedin, Master of Arcana and Divinity, is often referred to as the Master of Magic. A deity of all magic, divine and arcane, he's often also attributed almost all other forms of magic as well, including the hedge magics, nature magics and various other arts. Zedin is seen as a fairly abstract God with little personality and much power. It is often said that to ask a bargain of Zedin is to trade that which you don't know for that which you didn't intend.

Zedin is worshipped by most wizards, and called upon by most Clerics of all Orders, though many Clerics have a second deity they revere. Zedin is also called upon by those who wish a miracle to change their circumstance, and his name is the one most often used as a curse.

Zedin is revered throughout the year, but he is especially celebrated during the following festivals; The Day of the Seven, Ginshu and Laishu.

Vela, Lady of Fortune and Fate is a goddess whom almost all call upon in times of need, or when things don't seem to go their way. Often called Lady Luck, Vela is one of the most familiar and humanized of deities, and she's the favored Aspect by many.

Vela is worshipped by gamblers, thieves, clerics and merchants alike, and in the ever-more materialistic cities of Anarkesia, it is not uncommon to see her image depicted on all manner of businesses, casinos and banks. Vela is often called upon in gambling houses and sporting events to bless one with luck,

Vela is revered throughout the year, but she is especially celebrated during the following festivals; The Day of the Seven, Festival of Fortune and Swordsmeet, though she's often mentioned during Wintertide preparations as well.

Perceus, Lord of Peace and Passion, is often depicted with flame and water. Seen as the God of Emotions, it is not uncommon to hear Perceus blamed for one with a hot temper, or mentioned in reference when speaking of someone with unusual calm or inner peace. Whether called upon during meditation or sworn by during acts of violence, Perceus is a very humanized deity, despite his depiction being one of the least familiar.

Perceus is worshipped by orders of peace and tranquility and various meditative sects, and also by paladins of vengeance and war deities. Perceus is one of the Seven with the most contrast between his Facets, and worship of only one Facet is more common than with most other Gods.

Perceus is revered throughout the year, but he is especially celebrated during the following festivals; The Day of the Seven, Shinrah, Meadfest, Rapture, Firefest and Swordsmeet.

Karazan, Maestro of Music and Mischief, is favoured by many for his musical aspect, even though his Mischief aspect is most commonly ascribed to criminals and the like. The beloved God of Bards and Rogues alike, Karazan has been known to interfere directly with the mortal realm more than any of the other Seven, albeit in smaller manners. It is often believed that Karazan takes more interest in the activities of mortals than the other members of the Seven.

Karazan is worshipped by orders of music and laughter and many different joyful churches. He is also followed diligently by many of the darker members of society and is often asked to forgive those who have transgressed against the law.

Karazan is revered throughout the year, but he is especially celebrated during the following festivals; The Day of the Seven, Rapture and the Festival of Dance, though there is usually music at most festivals, so one might argue he's always called upon during times of happiness and celebration.

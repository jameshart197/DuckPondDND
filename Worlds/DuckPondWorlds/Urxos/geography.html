Geography of urxos

Anarkesia was once a land of volcanic rock and barren wasteland. The Anarkesians, however, are the foremost architects of Urxos, and have transformed this otherwise inhospitable landscape into a true wonder of technology. With the three cities growing quickly enough that the borders almost touch, this country is a land of roads, skyscrapers, and commerce. The populace was originally human, but these days you can find as many warforged as you can humans, and almost as many tourists and immigrants as natives. The language of Anarkesia is primarily common, although there are many speakers of other languages within the cities, as a result of the influx of people from other territories.

Tethoris

Tethoris is a vast metropolis in its own right, with a skyline that will make you dizzy as you walk through the streets. It is very common while walking through the bustling highstreets of Tethoris to see many tourists walking around, staring at the sky. This technological giant of a city has turned Electrolosis into the most common of resources, meaning even those that live in poverty on the outskirts still have access to automated lights and heating. Tethoris has a criminal underworld as huge as the city above it, where illicit goods from all over the world can be purchased as well as the unusual Electrolosis innovations used to access new heights of hedonism and indulgence.

Lextor

Lextor is home to the Sezencar, thus meaning that as much of the city is in the heights, accessible only by these vehicles, as is on the ground. At ground level the city is home to wide avenues and meandering boulevards; this does not, however, take away from the fact that there may well be a motorway above your head. The permanence of the pollution here and the presence of continuous city lights leads to a strange skyline of gold and blue.

Taxlington

Taxlington is the industrial capital of Anarkesia, with Electrolosis factories and industrial buildings widespread throughout the city. As such, the residents are less wealthy than those of neighbouring cities, and many a business owner here will keep a home and his family in Lextor or Tethoris. Under a permanent smog of pollution, the night sky in Taxlington is as red as it is black.

Implants, Upgrades and Augments

In Anarkesia, personal technological enhancements can be broken down into three categories; implants, upgrades and augments. Implants are mental enhancements that allow the user to do things they otherwise would be unable to do, such as casting spells or telepathic communication. Upgrades are enhancements to the already existing abilities of a user, such as increased strength or capacity to cast spells within a day. Augments are physical upgrades or replacements to parts of the body, such as cybernetic limbs or artificial eyes.

Almost every humanoid citizen in Anarkesia has three technological implants. These are as follows; An ICD (internal communication device) An ATEC (augmentation and temporal enhancement chip) An AUD (accessible upload drive) In addition to these, every citizen has a way of storing and paying with credits, more information on these technologies can be found in the economy section.

ICD

An ICD is a device implanted within the brain that allows individuals to connect socially and telepathically. This ranges from brief messages to people in your ‘contacts’ via voice, to the ability to transmit complex schematics or vivid imagery to a group of people within the same room. Individuals without an ICD can replicate some of the features using PCIs (personal communication interfaces, usually found within personal computers or other computational devices)

ATEC

Any individual with upgrades or implants must have an ATEC. This surgically-placed neurological implant modifies the functionality and capacity of the brain. Through use of an ATEC, an individual may gain access to abilities they previously did not have, or enhance those already present.

AUD

An AUD is a small interface, the port for which is placed on the neck, allows for entire libraries or databases of information to be given to the user instantly. That individual will then be able to recall anything from that information on demand, without delay. It should be noted that while the theory of something* can be imparted via AUD, practical application must still be practised and learned. (Yes, you can upload the knowledge of kung-fu to someone, but it won’t necessarily allow them to fight like Bruce Lee or Neo.)

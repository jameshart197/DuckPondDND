The History of Urxos

Before everything there was peace. The world lived happily, or as happily as any world does. There was racial diversity, and the world was a place of magic and beauty.

In the time before ages, the inhabitants of this world lived in relative harmony. Companionship blossomed between the various races. Then came The Morgoth, the Original Evil, a Demon of great power. While other Obyrith (Evil Deities, see section 2C) were focussed purely on chaos and destruction, and were subsequently being removed from the Abyss by various other races, The Morgoth came to the material plane to escape destruction, but also because his nature was different. He planned, and he planned long term. Unlike other demons, he did not require wanton destruction, instead desiring domination. His efforts were calculated. Upon witnessing the arrival of The Morgoth, Ilvandel, God of Balance, created Shinshei, or Divinity, an immortal being on the material plane to balance The Morgoth. Thus ended the First Age.

At first, neither Morgoth nor Shinshei affected the mortal plane directly, choosing instead to watch over from afar. Over time, The Morgoth began to gain an occult following in the bowels of the nether depths. As dwarves often will, they dug too deep, revealing The Morgoth’s presence to the races that inhabit the Underdark. Slowly the temptation of power that The Morgoth offered was enough to convert many to his following, and his power in this world grew. The Shinshei, however, recruited the kings of the goodly races. Soon a subtle war was taking place, followers being converted, and occasionally removed. Thus began the Third Age.

As time passed, this developed into a complete split of Underworld and Overworld. But The Morgoth was a temptation too great to deny for many of the so-called Goodly races, in particular Gnomes and Humans. Many were drawn to the promise of power, and The Morgoth's influence started to reach the Overworld. To balance this, the Shinshei bestowed gifts of great power upon the goodly races; most of all the Elves.

Seeing this, The Morgoth allowed minor demons to come through the gates of the Abyss, a scourge upon the earth. So the Shinshei created Sylan, a land of pure paradise where his people would thrive. The Sylan was untouchable to The Morgoth, stretching halfway across the continent of Urxos. Over time, however, the corruption of The Morgoth spread across the boundaries. It was, in the end, not the Morgoth’s agents, but temptation that reduced Sylan to a dark corner of the world. Humans began to transform into something different, something darker. They discovered the process of Electrolosis, creating technology that used magic but was not magical in and of itself. As Electrolosis spread, so did the influence of The Morgoth. Apart from a few places of purity and justice, The Morgoth’s influence was now everywhere.

So then began the Fourth Age. The age of technology. The age of balance. As The Morgoth had finally wiped out the majority of the Goodly Races from the earth, the Shinshei underwent a transition. Abandoning the purest of his morals, the Shinshei became involved in mortal politics, and in the use of Electrolosis. With this newfound power, the Shinshei was able to discreetly convert those of more flexible morals to keeping the goodly peace, and the two entered an agreement of peace, at least for now. But the balance is precarious. It may take only one domino tile to fall for the entire peace to collapse.

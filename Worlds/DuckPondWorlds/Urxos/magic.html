Magic in Urxos

Magic is very commonplace in Urxos, despite technology replicating a lot of its primary uses. Magic is still used for its convenience, simplicity and accessibility. However, whilst there is a lot of lower level magic, outside of the Mystix, there are not many high level practictioners of magic in Anarkesia, at least above the ground. In Perdition it may be more common to find very specifically talented magic users, such as Demons, Celestials, or practitioners of Necromancy or Divination.

In terms of the upper city, whilst most citizens can lay claim to a cantrip or two, very few will be capable of casting anything above third level. And much magic has been replaced entirely by technology; almost nobody is capable of using spells such as Fly, Invisibility, Shield, Longstrider, or healing magic, as technology has replaced these arts completely.

There are also many magic users who specialize in things that enhance their day to day work. You may find entertainers incorporating illusions into their work for example, or various presenters making use of magic to enhance their style. There are others, such as journalists using magic to record information, chefs using a mage hand to hold a dish or business executives using illusions to demonstrate concepts who use magic as a by-product of their work without much thought.

Substantial Changes to Magic

There are many elements of magic that may be different in the world of Urxos, or in Anarkesia specifically. There are also laws, changes in logic and adjustments to certain spells. This information is documented below;

Truesight

It is worth noting that many humanoid individuals in Anarkesia will have implants giving them the ability to see through magical darkness or see individuals who are under the effects of invisibility. It is worth mentioning that this does not allow them to see through illusions.

Charm Person (and some similiar spells)

Most charm effects will not require a save from the enemy, but instead require a charisma check from you to disarm a person or cause them to cease their aggression. This may lead to the person being more civil towards you, speaking more freely, or even spilling secrets. People affected by this spell are not aware of it. Direct charms (or commands) still work as prior, but some humanoids may have implements assisting them in resisting these effects.

Summoning

Due to the prevalence of Demons, Devils and Celestials upon the material plane, “Summon” spells work very differently in Urxos. Instead of demanding that a being come forth, and it being completely under your control, you instead open a gate to another plane and make a request or interact with a creature. As a result of this the Mystix have banned the usage of all summons and inter-planar gates within cities, and breaking these rules can result in the eradication of magic from within your body. Nevertheless, summoners do exist and continue to thrive by making bonds with those that they summon.

Necromancy

In contrast, Necromancy is not illegal, though greatly frowned upon. This being the process of reanimating the dead, or causing a corpse to rise. Creatures who had cybernetic enhancements in life may retain them in death, and certain creatures reanimated may retain many of their abilities from life, and be more resistant to your control.

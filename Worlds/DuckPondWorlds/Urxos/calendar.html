Calendar

The calendar of Urxos is based on 10 day weeks. Each month has three weeks. The year is split over 12 months, each of these 30 days. On the 1st and 15th of each month there is a festival day. Some festivals are much more closely observed than others, but these festivals are noted throughout Urxos. One festival might see a lot more celebration in some countries than others, but most festivals are at least given some attention. In Anarkesia it is very typical of high labour jobs to give 1 day's holiday for the Mid-Festival and 2 days holiday for the End-Festival, resulting in 3 days off per month, the 1st, 15th and 30th.

Festivals of Xandros

The Day of the Seven Ammah 15 A festival devoted to the Seven, the Seven Gods of Urxos, with their twin facets. On this day all Seven are revered and worshipped, and a devotion is made to each God. Even those who do not attend regular worship still tend to make an offering to each of the Seven on this day.
Shinrah Ammah 30 A day of introspection, meditation and preparation for the coming Spring. This is more strongly observed in the Kinschiro Republic, but has become very fashionable in Anarkesia in recent years amongst the richer populace.
Rainsmeet Seraph 15 Also known as the festival of fertility. Traditionally this is when the seeds are planted and the farmers give prayer and hope for the forthcoming year, but it is often a day celebrated sensually and romantically in the major cities
Meadfest Seraph 30 A day for drinking, all over the world. Whilst one of the least religious festivals, with very few remembering the origins, it is one of the most widely celebrated throughout Urxos
The Festival of Fortune Shylah 15 Traditionally a day to give thanks for what you have, and pray for prosperity, it has become a day to game and gamble in most big cities. If you work a well paid job, there is a traditional yearly bonus paid on the Festival of Fortune.
The Day of Rebirth Shylah 30 A celebration of rebirth and renewal, this festival is a day for people to make amends with those they have fallen out with. Traditionally dead wood is burnt in a large bonfire, so that new birth can blossom in its wake.
Rapture Palesun 15 A celebration of hedonism, pleasure and luxury, Rapture is a time for music, singing and traditionally, a masqued ball. Rapture has always been celebrated globally, but the manner in which it is done is vastly different from country to country. In Anarkesia its often a time of liberating parties with very few boundaries and a lot of reckless abandon.
Festival of the Twin Moons Palesun 30 A celebration of Sylar and Ondar, the Twin moons of Urxos. This festival is celebrated in different ways all over the world, but in Anarkesia, its traditional to turn off all Electrolosis or Magical lighting and rely only on candle light in the evening, and is a time for quiet meals with loved ones.
Festival of Dance Ariel 15 A beloved festival by most, the Festival of Dance has become as much about the huge broadcasts that go out each year with dozens of famous popstars and dancers as it is about the festival itself. It seems fewer people each year are dancing, and far more are watching others dance.
The Long Goodbye Ariel 30 The Long Goodbye is also known as the Festival of the Departing. It is a time to say goodbye to loved ones lost throughout the year, and reminisce about those who passed over long ago. Many observe this festival by staying up throughout the night, often keeping a fire burning as they do so.
All Saints Day Pyrrus 15 A day to remember all the varying patron Saints, many of whom are forgotten now, but various ones who are remembered. Saints are, traditionally, religious figures of the past who did something great. But often Saints are related to certain things (Such as a Patron Saint of Love, or of Food, etc.)
Firefest Pyrrus 30 A celebreation of flame and fire, this is also known as Midsummer's Eve. Late into the evening fire shows, fireworks, bonfires and such are commonplace alongside music, dancing and magical displays.
Festival of Life Firewane 15 A celebration of life and birth, and time to give thanks. This festival is usually celebrated during the day with long lunches with friends and family. It is traditional to give gifts to your parents if they are still in your life on this day.
Swordsmeet Firewane 30 Once a gladitorial competition, Swordsmeet is often a day for gaming and challenges in the modern era. Some countries still celebrate with traditional wrestling or fighting tournaments, but it is far more common to see sporting events and the like now.
Harvest Gala Lowsun 15 A celebration of the harvest, this is far more important to less developed countries or more rural areas, where farming is possible. In modern cities, there are often market stalls and people often bring goods they have baked or cooked themselves.
Ginshu Lowsun 30 A festival celebrating learning and knowledge, this is celebrated in different ways all over the world. In Anarkesia it is traditional to exchange gifts of books or other learning devices, and many interesting seminars are held throughout the cities.
Day of the Dragon Zerahm 15 The Day of the Dragon is an ancient festival that is still celebrated worldwide. It usually involves a procession or carnival with dragon theming, and many exotic or interesting costumes. Recently, in Anarkesia, it has also become traditional to let off Dragon-themed fireworks or magical displays in the sky.
Hunter's Call Zerahm 30 An old festival where people used to gather to hunt, it is still celebrated in that way in more rural areas. In the big cities, where it is rare that anyone leaves the city, there is a popular activity in the youth that involves a glorified version of Hide and Seek throughout the City.
Laishu Sunsebb 15 Otherwise known as the Festival of Magic, Laishu is a day for celebrating magic and the impossible. Often full of wondrous displays, market stalls of unusual and exotic magical goods, and tricksters and illusionists, this day is beloved by most children, and remains fascinating to many in their later years too.
Festival of Lights Sunsebb 30 The Festival of Lights is a day of joy and festivity, mostly taking place in the evening. Whether its letting small candles float on the river, letting lanterns into the sky or taking part in huge light festivals involving magic, it is an eyecatching event all around Urxos.
Creation Day Rodah 15 Traditionally celebrated as the "day the world was made", this is a time for remembering the Old Gods, before the coming of the Seven. In older times, everyone celebrated their birthday on Creation Day as opposed to the day they were born on, but this has fallen out of fashion.
Feast of Spirits Rodah 30 A time when the Spirit Realm is believed to be closer to the mortal realm, this is a day for Ghosts and Haunted Tales and spooky costumes. Despite the many themed parties and children's games, this day has some scientific relevance, and anyone seeking to resurrect a lost friend or family member will often do so on the Feast of Spirits.
Wintertide Fest Fellnight 15 A yearly festival of gift giving and game playing, this is the most widely celebrated festival globally. Much like Christmas on Earth, but without any religious connotations, its a time for materialism and family to meet in a glorious celebration of marketing, gaudy decorations and awkward dinners.
Year's End Fellnight 30 Celebrated Globally, Year's End is much like New Year's Eve on Earth, a festival of partying, drinking and fireworks. There are traditions in various different countries, but in Anarkesia the traditions are mostly drinking and fireworks.

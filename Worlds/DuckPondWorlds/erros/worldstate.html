State of the World

In Trevath, there has been peace for over 100 years, with very few conflicts on any borders. Silvercrest is home to the great Paladin King Darius and the Trevathion Shield whose knights keep the peace within the land. The Lords and Ladies, Dukes and Duchesses and other Nobility are his loyal subjects, and rule smaller areas of land on his behalf, and the smallfolk pay vassalage to them in return. Taxes are fairly low, the land is rich in resources and with almost no border trouble Trevath is as prosperous as it has ever been.

The Untamed Lands to the South are much as the name implies - although the Lich-King Malakai has been dead over 100 years, his legacy remains in the form of broken land, terrifying monsters and latent dark magics. There are many ruins and abandoned keeps that the adventurous have attempted to excavate, often resulting in their demise. There are hoards of treasures untold hidden within these lands, but they are truly dangerous even to the hardiest of parties. The further South one travels, the more dangerous it gets, and nobody knows if there is civilization somewhere further South than these lands.

In and around Trevath are the lands of Sunbaar and Arugat to the East, home to seafaring folk and island dwellers, as well as the dragonfolk of Sunbaar. Whilst there is little conflict on the borders, often due to the barrier of the Greenwood between the lands, there is little friendship either. Locailun to the north is home to the hardier Northern folk, who regularly come to trade their unusual goods in Ramsey and even Silvercrest. Whilst the mountains and ice plains do not have much in the way of cities and sophistication, its said that the wonders are many and varied.

Wardon, although an independent nation, could easily be mistaken as part of Trevath. Those that live there are peaceful and friendly folk for the most part, and the separation is mainly due to their unusual religious beliefs, following the Totemic beliefs of old. Many of the folk here are magical in nature, and a little unusual to Trevathions.

To the West, Gorvaz is the Ancestral Homeland of the Dwarves, and there are many grand citadels within the land. Overground the terrain can be almost unpassable at times, being hugely mountainous and exceptionally dry, though folk do live in thewse lands. To the South of this is Vasana, a fair land home to many Elves, including the Sea Elves. Whilst they are very welcoming of travelers, very few who are not Elves choose to stay in Vasana's lands.

Playable Races in Erros

Elves

High Elves on DNDBeyond

Oft proud and haughty, High Elves can often trace their bloodlines back for many millennia. Many have found their way into the nobility or other seats of power of various cities with their ancient wealth and endless patience

Wood Elves on DNDBeyond

Wood Elves tend to be a proud but quiet folk they describe themselves as closest to the Earth of all the Elves and are often reproachful of those who adopt the city lifestyle. They spend much of their time singing, dancing and worshipping nature but do not be fooled; most Wood Elves are deadly swordsmen and exceptional archers with natural magic that they can bring to bear.

Dwarves on DNDBeyond

A clannish and independent group, Dwarves have their homes in mountains and hills all over the world, though they keep themselves mostly to themselves. They are insular and solitary and not particularly welcoming of outsiders, though never truly turning anyone in need from their doorsteps. They are notoriously racist however, and will always value the lives of Dwarves above any others. They tend to stick to their large underground cities, though some dwarves have chosen to venture forth from these citadels to visit the wider world.

Mountain Dwarves and Hill Dwarves are the variants of Dwarves notable within Trevath. Mountain Dwarves tend to be a gruff and cynical people, and take time to trust and even longer to forgive. They are often adventurous in their younger years and are traditionally fairly martial in nature. Hill Dwarves tend to be a little more materialistic and ritualistic, valuing themselves and others by what they possess and their wealth and reputation. They tend to be deeply conservative, rooting their values and beliefs in traditions that have survived for millennia even as the world changes around them. From infancy, Hill Dwarves are taught that their life is determined by tradition, from what their profession should be to who they should marry.

Gnomes on DNDBeyond

Gnomes are spread throughout the world, and can vary greatly in their nature; nevertheless there is one trait that can link them all together, and that is curiosity. Gnomes are often found as students of the various arts, be they magical or scientific. They are a people who love to create, and to build, and their constructions are often incredibly complicated and magnificent to behold.

The Forest Gnomes tend towards a more peaceful life, and are usually found dwelling on the edges of forests. The Rock Gnomes tend towards the crystalline groves at the foothills of mountains, and tend to be more of the magical arts.

Humans on DNDBeyond

The predominant race in Trevath many an Elf has been heard to say that Humans are conquering the world merely with their breeding habits. Regardless, it is certainly true that in Trevath there are more humans than any other peoples. As varied as the plant life or animal life of the world, little can be said to generalise humans except to note that they live their lives with great passion and haste; this may be due to the shortness of their lives. Humans are all over the world, and are as varied as any race can be.

Humans have been capable of the most heinous of evil crimes and the greatest of noble deeds; they are one of the few races that tolerate poverty, and allow members of their own race to starve and go homeless within their vast cities. Most Human cities are multicultural and diverse, as most humans are very open-minded and accepting of others, despite the way they isolate members of their own communities. Their cities are led by nobility and bloodline is often an important factor in the fate of a human. It can be impossible to judge the nature of any human by their race alone; they follow many Gods, many traditions and varied beliefs depending on where in the world they are born, their class, stature and wealth.

Half-Elves on DNDBeyond

A blend of the long-lived Elves and the shorter, hastier humankind, Half-Elves bring traits of both. Half-Elves are as varied as humans and much of their heritage and culture is dependent on which Elven subrace they draw their ancestry from. They are shunned by neither of their parent races and fit well into most societies, their fine features and gentle natures often blending well with their human natural charisma. Each Half-Elf is different depending on which group of Elves and Humans they were born to, and they are as diverse as humans or Elves in that respect.

Halflings on DNDBeyond

Halflings are affable and cheerful people, they cherish the bonds of family and friendship as well as the comforts of hearth and home, harbouring few dreams of gold or glory.

Even the most adventurous among them usually venture into the world for reasons of community, friendship, wanderlust, or curiosity. They love discovering new things, even simple things, such as an exotic food or an unfamiliar style of clothing. Halflings make excellent merchants and negotiators and many have worked up the wealth to gain great importance within the cities they live in.

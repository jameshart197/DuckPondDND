Magic in Erros

Magic in Trevath is not particularly unusual; there are many practictioners of the Arcane, though most are small time tricksters, apprentice wizards or those who have learnt a small amount of the Arcane to assist in their daily lives (tradesfolk learning things such as magehand or mold earth for example).

Those with more Arcane power tend to be wizards who have spent long years researching their arts and gathering spells to their spellbooks. Those to whom magic comes innately, usually referred to as sorcerers, are fairly unusual, and those who recieve the Arcane magics in other ways are few and far between. With that being said, there are many non-humanoids who claim the Arcane Arts as part of their nature, though they aren't all that common to see.

There are many churches, temples and other places of worship where one might encounter priests with the ability to cast Divine Magic spells, at least to some extent - Rare is the priest that can cast higher magic such as Greater Restoration, or even raising the dead. The Trevathion Shield are mostly Paladins who claim some level of Divine casting, and there are many Rangers, Druids and others who can tap at least some level of magic.

As such, magic is not regarded with fear or surprise, at least at lower power levels (those that can cast spells of up to second level) however more powerful casters may garner some reputation and become known to authorities in the land. In the Untamed Lands, it is expected you will be able to cast some level of magic to venture in, and you will most certainly encounter others who can do so, both friend, foe and monster.

Summoning

It is considered inappropriate to summon creatures of a monstrous nature, or any whom are demon, devil or undead, within civilized lands. If you do have a servant of an unusual nature, it is appropriate to ask them to wait for you somewhere away from villages and towns. That being said, it is expected that you will use any and all powers available to you whilst in the Untamed Lands.

Necromancy

Necomrancy, specifically the process of reanimating the dead, or causing a corpse to rise, is considered distasteful in civilized lands, and illegal within bigger towns and cities. It is not uncommon for a temple practitioner to speak with the dead, but to raise them to a state of undeath is almost unanimously feared, due to the nature of Malakai and the legacy he left behind.

Changes to specific spells

The following spells have been removed/banned

  • Silvery Barbs
  • Comprehend Languages
  • Identify
  • Tongues
  • Distort Value
  • Protection from Ballistics
  • Modify Memory

Detect Magic

When casting this spell, it will only reveal the presence of magic in general. An Arcana check (and perhaps others) will be needed to discern anything further.

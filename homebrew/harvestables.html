Harvestable Ingredients

Ingredients can be divided into those that can be grown or found and those that can be harvested from creatures. Listed below are the harvested ingredients that can be procured from animals, beasts and monsters. For a list of those that can be grown or found please see Alchemical Ingredients. Most alchemical ingredients have one primary purpose, but sometimes an ingredient can produce an unusual effect when mixed in a certain way. Not all properties are known for all alcemical ingredients, but details are given where possible.

Animal Bone on DNDBeyond

Animal Bones are a staple in any Alchemist's ingredient collection. They are often used for hardening to create oils, thickening when ground or for filtering recipes down. Whilst most animal bones have no innate properties of their own, they are a crucial part of the alchemical process.

Properties include

  • Hardening agent

Animal Carapace on DNDBeyond

Animal Carapaces are a staple in any Alchemist's ingredient collection. They are often used for containing highly volatile creations, or keeping parts of an alchemical bomb separate from others. Whilst most animal carapaces have no innate properties of their own, they are a crucial part of the alchemical process.

Properties include

  • Separation
  • Containment

Animal Fat on DNDBeyond

Animal Fats are another constant in an Alchemist's stores. They are often used for insulation to stopper hot liquids, separate hot liquids, or to contain icy freezing concoctions. Whilst most animal Fats have no innate properties of their own, they are a crucial part of the alchemical process.

Properties include

  • Insulation

Animal Horn on DNDBeyond

Animal Horns are another staple in the Alchemist's kitchen. They are often primarily used for focusing an ingredient into a specific part of the mixture, or for concentrating a solution Whilst most Animal Horns have no innate properties of their own, some can be incredibly powerful ingredients in their own right, so it is vital to check the properties of the horn you are using before including it in your potions.

Properties include

  • Focus
  • Concentration

Animal Skin on DNDBeyond

Animal Skins are vast and varied, and most can be used in one way or another so it is not unusual to see an Alchemist carrying different bottled samples stored in vinegar. Animal Skins can have many different properties and are as varied as the animals they come from. That being said, almost any animal skin can be used as a bladder or container for an impact-based solution that is designed to shatter when thrown. Animal Skins can also be used to separate two parts of a concoction until impact. There are also various poisons that are filtered through animal skins to remove taste or texture from a liquid without losing potency.

Properties include

  • Storage
  • Separation
  • Filtration

Animal Tooth on DNDBeyond

Animal Teeth are a staple of the Alchemist's Kit. They are the best of funnels when a small hole is bored into the tip, and can be used for reducing and distilling. It can also be a great way of filtering out the thicker parts or solids from a liquid. Animal teeth can often have magical properties of their own when they belong to mightier beasts, included sharpening, cleansing and cutting.

Properties include

  • Distillation Process
  • Sharpening
  • Cutting
  • Cleansing

Animal Eyeball on DNDBeyond

The Eyeball of an Animal can be used for a number of different things. Whilst some are completely useless, the majority of them hold some small amount of magic that allows them to be used in the processes of divination. Whether in scrying, observing or casting auguries, the eyeballs of animals have been used in the process for centuries. Some eyes have individual properties however, so it is always worth checking this before using them in your distillations. It might be noted that some darker poisons create hallucinations and visions, and those draughts often contain eyeballs.

Properties include

  • Divination
  • Hallucinogenic

Bat Wing on DNDBeyond

Bat's Wings have been used in recipes for countless centuries, most commonly used to brew potions to alter ones' own appearance. As long as you can get over the texture, they are always useful ingredients.

Properties include

  • Disguise
  • Self Alteration

Beholder's Eye on DNDBeyond

A rare and difficult to obtain ingredient, an eye from a Beholder can be an invaluable ingredient. Used in potions to grant truesight, or alter sight in various different ways, they can be extremely expensive to obtain.

Properties include

  • True Sight
  • Darkvision
  • Divination
  • Antimagic

Bird Feather on DNDBeyond

Bird Feathers are another constant in an Alchemist's stores. They are often used for filtration and separating in the distillation process, but some feathers have the ability to bestow weightlessness or even flight.

Properties include

  • Separation Agent
  • Filtration
  • Flight
  • Weightlessness

Boar's Tusk on DNDBeyond

Boar's Tusks can be very difficult to prepare, although very easy to obtain on the current market or by hunting them down yourself. The tusk must be ground into a fine powder. It can be dangerous to consume too much of this powder for most humanoids, but in small quantities it can increase resilience.

Properties include

  • Enhances Wisdom
  • Strong Poison

Carrion Crawler's Gland on DNDBeyond

Although difficult to obtain, and very messy to use, this ingredient is extremely marketable. Rarely used for its more complex uses, its more commonly used as an enhancement to ones' stamina and virility. A favourite amongst the Nobility.

Properties include

  • Enhanced Stamina
  • Virility
  • Energy

Dragon's Blood on DNDBeyond

Said to grant agelessness, this single ingredient is the reason countless adventurers have died at the claws of dragons all over the world. It may or may not have other magical properties, but few enough alchemists have had the opportunity to work with this legendary substance that little is truly known.

Properties include

  • As yet unknown

Dragon's Heart on DNDBeyond

Said to be the "universal cure", a Dragon's heart is immeasurable in worth. Alongside the rumours surrounding the blood of a Dragon, this is why countless adventurers will still try and slay a dragon inside its' lair.

Properties include

  • As yet unknown

Dragon's Scale on DNDBeyond

A legendary and difficult to obtain ingredient, not much is known about the properties of Dragon's Scales in potions, but what is known is they must be stored in pure alcohol as soon as they are harvested or they quickly fade and lose their power.

Properties include

  • As yet unknown

Dragon's Tooth on DNDBeyond

A legendary ingredient about which only myth states its' potential uses, said to do various things from give one the power to breathe as a dragon might to increased strength, some myths even say a well brewed potion containing a Dragon's Tooth may grant the imbiber the ability to consume anything and find it nutritious, though most herbalists say this is nothing but hearsay and fantasy.

Properties include

  • As yet unknown

Eagle's Faeather on DNDBeyond

Granting an instantaneous improvement to sight, the feather of an Eagle is a staple in various stimulants provided by alchemists to enhance the natural abilities of trackers and rangers.

Properties include

  • Enhancing Vision
  • Darkvision

Elemental Dust on DNDBeyond

The dust remaining when an elemental of any kind dissipates has various uses and is a staple in the pouch of many alchemists. Used both to create fanciful effects such as fire breath, and to negate the effects of various other elemental damage.

Properties include

  • Various Elemental Effects

Ettercap Silk on DNDBeyond

This thin, silvery substance is one of the most valuable substances by weight in existence. Known to provide resilience and strength, and near weightless, it is as valuable to the herbalist as it is difficult to retrieve and store safely.

Properties include

  • Weightlessness
  • Resilience
  • Strength

Harpy Eye on DNDBeyond

The key ingredient of a Philter of Love, this is something always in high demand. It is said to have darker properties, but thus far that is unproven.

Properties include

  • Love
  • Possession

Hydra Heart on DNDBeyond

An extremely rare and valuable ingredient, even consuming it without preparation can grant unusual properties to the imbiber; prepared properly by a talented alchemist, this is a truly versatile and powerful tool.

Properties include

  • Potion Strengthening
  • Potion Alteration

Ivory Tusk on DNDBeyond

Ivory Tusks are usually used in other crafts, such as jewellery making or wood carving, but they do have simplistic alchemical properties. They are exceptionally useful in the making of oils, as they boil down to a fine oil that can dissolve many ingredients within them. They are also able to grant courage in the darkest of times if prepared properly.

Properties include

  • Heroism
  • Conversion to Oil

Manticore Claws on DNDBeyond

Many experiments have been made with the claw of a manticore ground finely into powder and imbibed alongside other ingredients. What is known is that it will cause your nails to grow to claws of almost adamantine hardness, what isn't known is what else it does.

Properties include

  • Beastial Properties
  • Sharpening

Merfolk Scale on DNDBeyond

Many merfolk clans have been hunted to near extinction for their scales, providing the ability to breathe underwater as if one were a fish themselves. There are plant alternatives that whilst difficult to obtain are probably kinder to the gentle merfolk.

Properties include

  • Breathing Underwater
  • Swimming Enhancement
  • Shapechanging (limited)

Myconid Spores on DNDBeyond

Lethal in large quantities, these unusual spores can do all kinds of things to the human body, but what is most interesting about this ingredient is its ability to greatly enhance the effects of an already prepared potion.

Properties include

  • Potion Enhancement
  • Toxic
  • Body Alteration

Octopus Ink on DNDBeyond

A delicacy in some parts of the world, octopus ink has some powerful magical properties if prepared properly. You'll be tasting it for days after the potion is finished though.

Properties include

  • Divination
  • Shapechanging
  • Numbness
  • Paralysis

Pegasus Feather on DNDBeyond

The sole reason these magnificent beasts have been hunted to near extinction is the fact that their feathers can give a humanoid the ability to fly for a short period of time. It is perfectly possible to retrieve feathers from living Pegasi, and it is a far more sustainable method.

Properties include

  • Flight

Quaggoth Liver on DNDBeyond

If you can stomach both harvesting this ingredient and imbibing it, it can enhance your hardiness beyond what one might believe. Then again, you'd need to have a pretty good constitution just to stomach making the potion.

Properties include

  • Resilience
  • Resistance
  • Constitution

Quetzl Spine on DNDBeyond

The poison within a Quetzl Spine is vicious and painful, but if distilled properly it can be a cure for all but the most virulent of diseases. It also makes an excellent tea. The speed at which this toxin can paralyze or render senseless a foe is the reason poisoners value it so highly.

Properties include

  • Curative
  • Unconciousness
  • Paralysis

Remorhaz Egg on DNDBeyond

These eggs are invaluable in various potions that can make one resistant to the elements. Dangerous to obtain however, as Remorhaz are extremely protective of their young and their eggs.

Properties include

  • Elemental Resistance

Salamander Blood on DNDBeyond

The blood of a salamander burns hot even weeks after its harvesting. A most interesting ingredient, it can also be used to power lamps for months on end.

Properties include

  • Heat
  • Light
  • Ignition

Scorpion Sac on DNDBeyond

A staple basic in the poisoner's inventory, the pain that accompanies poisoning by a scorpion is most exquisite. It can be combined with other ingredients to cause unusual and interesting effects, but its primary purpose is simply pain. Milking a scorpion is a task best done by those most experienced and careful though.

Properties include

  • Toxicity
  • Truth Detection

Sea Hag's Heart on DNDBeyond

How the alchemical properties of a sea hag's heart were discovered, this herbalist does not want to know. Nevertheless, their effects are well documented, granting vitality and energy to all but the most sick of patients.

Properties include

  • Vitality
  • Energy
  • Awakening

Snake Venom on DNDBeyond

The potency of the snake venom depends entirely, of course, on the snake it is taken from. No poisoner would be caught without at least some form of snake venom in their stores.

Properties include

  • Toxicity

Snaketongue on DNDBeyond

Snake's Tongues are well known for their uses in spellcasting as a component. What is little known is that depending on the breed of snake, the tongue of a snake can be used to alter poisons and the time they require to take effect. There are also some recipes that call for a snake's tongue that call on the power of thunder storms, though these are incredibly difficult to prepare.

Properties include

  • Altering Poisons
  • Lightning Effects

Tarrasque Gizzard on DNDBeyond

The ultimate dream of most herbalists is to be able to experiment with this mythical ingredient. Said to "cure all ills, grant agelessness and bestow unlimited power" you'd have to assume its at least good for something! Not exactly easy to obtain though.

Properties include

  • As yet unknown

Unicorn Blood on DNDBeyond

Unicorn Blood is a legendary and mythical ingredient. Said to restore forgotten youth, and cure disease, it is extremely rare and difficult to obtain.

Properties include

  • Curative
  • Youthfulness

Unicorn Hair on DNDBeyond

The hair of a unicorn is said to imbue someone with both speed and dexterity. It is very difficult to get hold of and there may be simpler options.

Properties include

  • Speed
  • Dexterity
  • Energy

Unicorn Heart on DNDBeyond

An extremely rare ingredient, the heart of a unicorn is said to imbue exquisite beauty upon the imbiber. These rumours are not verified however, but have derived from the saying that is fairly common in smaller villages, "She ate the heart of a Unicorn". Whether there is truth to these rumours, or other magical properties within the heart of a Unicorn, is yet to be verified.

Properties include

  • Beauty

Wyvern Eye on DNDBeyond

The eye of the wyvern isn't an easy thing to obtain, especially whole. However, its extensive uses in magical potions makes it invaluable to alchemists of all levels of experience.

Properties include

  • Emulsifier
  • Bonding Agent
  • Strengthening Poisons/Potions
  • Divination

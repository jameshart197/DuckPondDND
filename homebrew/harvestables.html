Harvestable Ingredients

Ingredients can be divided into those that can be grown or found and those that can be harvested from creatures. Listed below are the harvested ingredients that can be procured from animals, beasts and monsters. For a list of those that can be grown or found please see Alchemical Ingredients. Most alchemical ingredients have one primary purpose, but sometimes an ingredient can produce an unusual effect when mixed in a certain way. Not all properties are known for all alcemical ingredients, but details are given where possible.

Animal Bone on DNDBeyond

Animal Bones are a staple in any Alchemist's ingredient collection. They are often used for hardening to create oils, thickening when ground or for filtering recipes down. Whilst most animal bones have no innate properties of their own, they are a crucial part of the alchemical process.

Properties include

  • Hardening agent

Animal Carapace on DNDBeyond

Animal Carapaces are a staple in any Alchemist's ingredient collection. They are often used for containing highly volatile creations, or keeping parts of an alchemical bomb separate from others. Whilst most animal carapaces have no innate properties of their own, they are a crucial part of the alchemical process.

Properties include

  • Separation
  • Containment

Animal Fat on DNDBeyond

Animal Fats are another constant in an Alchemist's stores. They are often used for insulation to stopper hot liquids, separate hot liquids, or to contain icy freezing concoctions. Whilst most animal Fats have no innate properties of their own, they are a crucial part of the alchemical process.

Properties include

  • Insulation

Animal Horn on DNDBeyond

Animal Horns are another staple in the Alchemist's kitchen. They are often primarily used for focusing an ingredient into a specific part of the mixture, or for concentrating a solution Whilst most Animal Horns have no innate properties of their own, some can be incredibly powerful ingredients in their own right, so it is vital to check the properties of the horn you are using before including it in your potions.

Properties include

  • Focus
  • Concentration

Animal Skin on DNDBeyond

Animal Skins are vast and varied, and most can be used in one way or another so it is not unusual to see an Alchemist carrying different bottled samples stored in vinegar. Animal Skins can have many different properties and are as varied as the animals they come from. That being said, almost any animal skin can be used as a bladder or container for an impact-based solution that is designed to shatter when thrown. Animal Skins can also be used to separate two parts of a concoction until impact. There are also various poisons that are filtered through animal skins to remove taste or texture from a liquid without losing potency.

Properties include

  • Storage
  • Separation
  • Filtration

Animal Tooth on DNDBeyond

Animal Teeth are a staple of the Alchemist's Kit. They are the best of funnels when a small hole is bored into the tip, and can be used for reducing and distilling. It can also be a great way of filtering out the thicker parts or solids from a liquid. Animal teeth can often have magical properties of their own when they belong to mightier beasts, included sharpening, cleansing and cutting.

Properties include

  • Distillation Process
  • Sharpening
  • Cutting
  • Cleansing

Animal Eyeball on DNDBeyond

The Eyeball of an Animal can be used for a number of different things. Whilst some are completely useless, the majority of them hold some small amount of magic that allows them to be used in the processes of divination. Whether in scrying, observing or casting auguries, the eyeballs of animals have been used in the process for centuries. Some eyes have individual properties however, so it is always worth checking this before using them in your distillations. It might be noted that some darker poisons create hallucinations and visions, and those draughts often contain eyeballs.

Properties include

  • Divination
  • Hallucinogenic

Homebrew Rules

There are a variety of different homebrew rules we use during Duck Pond games. These are intended to increase the fun of the game, the ease of understanding and occasionally the difficulty.

  • Potions are a bonus action to use. Healing potions may be used as an action if you wish to gain the full amount of healing possible as opposed to rolling. You may also use your action to give someone else a potion, if conscious they must use their reaction to drink it.
  • A natural 20 on initiative will grant you advantage on your first action.
  • Haste grants a second “action of attacks” - if your character has 2 attacks as base, hasted they will have 4
  • Flanking is melee only. When 2 or more creatures that are not incapacitated surround an enemy they will have +2 to hit that creature.
  • You cannot hide in plain sight. Ever
  • Summons/pets/additionals always go immediately after their “owner”
  • Dice can be rolled on d&d beyond or in real life but please roll initiative on DNDBeyond
  • Tasha’s Optionals apply in all situations the player wishes them to (racials, class options etc.)
  • Cover grants armor class (2 for quarter, 4 for half, 6 for three quarter, 10 for full)
  • Hit points may be manually rolled - 1s are rerolled, 2s are rerolled for d10/12 hit die classes
  • Help requires proficiency OR a good explanation
  • Drawing/sheathing a weapon is a free action *almost* all the time (you may not draw and sheath the same weapon in a single turn as a free action)
  • Characters gain feats as they level (2,6,10,14,18) and must take stats when granted ASIs by standard rules
  • You may learn a proficiency. This takes time, and can take longer based on the proficiency in question and your current abilities (a high intelligence character might learn a language swiftly, but a low strength and constitution character may take a long time to adjust to wearing heavy armor)
  • When taking a feat, I would greatly prefer it if you take something that makes sense with your character in roleplay, as opposed to the strongest option. I also reserve the right to reject feat choices.
  • We will be using XP levelling, and XP can (and will) be granted for roleplay activities, combat victories, discoveries, general progress as characters, story progress or certain milestone achievements. All characters will always have the same amount of XP. Non-Combat solutions will often grant more XP but less loot!
  • Most important/valuable/strong magical items will use the Dormant/Slumbering/Awakened/Exalted progression system, allowing you to find cool items and then have them progress with you. Other magical items found will mostly be limited/single use items.
  • Any character can use a scroll. It requires an Arcana check (with your spellcasting stat in place of Intelligence if you are a spellcaster) to use a scroll, DC = 10 + spell level
  • Acrobatics can be used in place of Athletics to contest a grapple or escape, but not to initiate one (unless your class states otherwise)
  • In general, some skills may use alternate attributes where appropriate (Intimidation using Strength for example)
  • A surprise round is limited to a single action by a single player (this applies to enemies too!)
  • Inspiration Cards are going to be in use, and I will be giving them out during the game and sometimes at the end. These can be: Reroll with Advantage, add a D4/6/8 or Automatically Succeed. I would prefer players to avoid using more than one inspiration card in a single combat!
  • Please don’t draw on the map/images! It really does upset me!
  • Two people may exchange initiatives prior to combat. This is not usable on natural 1s or natural 20s and is only doable by a maximum of two people once per combat.
  • Failing three death saves (dying) allows for a “last hurrah” moment. This is an action that may be taken immediately but cannot be used to attempt to save one’s own life.
  • Jumping is a free action requiring half your movement speed, or a bonus action, players choice.
  • A melee character may choose to use a cleave attack, making attack rolls against all creatures in range. They may then split their damage evenly between the number of enemies hit.
  • Retreat is now an action, giving a combined Dash and Dodge effect, but only when moving directly away from opponents.
  • Lucky requires a reaction to change an opponent's roll, and is limited to once per turn for changing one's own rolls.
  • Having a “language” doesn’t necessarily mean you are fluent in it. But it might mean you know things about that culture, those people, or can understand historical references.
  • Bonus actions can be used in place of your action. This does not allow for two spell casts in one turn.
  • Dangerous criticals will be in play
  • Dropping to 0 hit points grants a level of exhaustion (after the combat) - this effect stacks.
  • A failed skill check can be repeated at the cost of a level of exhaustion. This roll is a single roll of the die, and does not include any disadvantage/advantage on the initial roll.
  • You may use your opportunity attack to grapple a creature instead of attack
  • Changed Exhaustion Rules: Each level of exhaustion grants a -1 to all d20 rolls, and the DC of your abilities/spell saves. At 5 levels of exhaustion, characters cannot take the dash action and short rests take 2 hours. If you exceed 10 levels of exhaustion (reach level 11) you die.
  • Natural 20s on saving throws and ability checks are always a success, though with ability checks it may grant a success in a different manner than expected.
  • A player may take two levels of exhaustion to cast a spell they have prepared if they have no remaining spell slots.
  • Deaths saves are made in private to the DM
